# Summary

Daniel Pereira's *Business Model Metrics and KPIs* is a comprehensive reference covering diagnostic and predictive metrics across SaaS, subscription, ecommerce, agency, affiliate, content, retail, open-source, and freemium business models. The guide distinguishes metrics (broad operational measurements) from KPIs (strategic indicators tied to goals), then deep-dives into acquisition, engagement, retention, and financial metrics. For SaaS specifically, it covers MRR/ARR, CAC, CLV, churn, NPS, LVR, viral coefficient, and customer health scoring. Each metric includes definition, formula, benchmarks, improvement tactics, and cross-functional relevance.

---

## How JAK should use this source

**Strong on:**
- Metric definitions and formulas for SaaS/subscription businesses
- Benchmarks and rule-of-thumb thresholds (e.g., good CAC, healthy churn)
- Improvement playbooks for each metric (practical levers)
- Cross-model comparison (SaaS vs. affiliate vs. agency vs. retail)

**Weak on:**
- Saudi-specific regulatory or tax treatment of recurring revenue
- Advanced statistical methods (cohort analysis beyond basics)
- Integration with modern data stacks (Snowflake, dbt, etc.)

**Best for:**
- Founders building dashboards and selecting their "north star" metrics
- ProcessBay engagements requiring metric-driven performance frameworks
- Utopian portfolio companies establishing their KPI architecture

**Not authoritative for:**
- Enterprise SaaS contract accounting (ASC 606 / IFRS 15)
- Hardware-as-a-Service unit economics
- Government reporting requirements
