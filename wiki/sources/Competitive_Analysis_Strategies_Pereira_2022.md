# Competitive Analysis Strategies

**Author:** Daniel Pereira (The Business Model Analyst, Ottawa, Canada)  
**Year:** 2022  
**Length:** ~122 pages  
**Genre:** Comprehensive competitive intelligence framework

## Summary

A thorough guide to competitive analysis structured as an end-to-end process: identify competitors (direct, indirect, substitute), gather intelligence across products, pricing, marketing, sales, and culture, then apply analytical frameworks to generate actionable insights. Covers foundational tools—SWOT, Strategic Group Analysis, Porter's Five Forces, Perceptual Mapping, BCG Matrix, and the Marketing Mix 7Ps—alongside practical guidance on feature matrices, market-share benchmarking, and ongoing competitive monitoring. Anchored by a detailed Sony vs. Nintendo case study.

## How JAK should use this source

- **Strong on:** Structured competitive analysis process; multi-dimensional comparison (product, price, marketing, culture); practical application of classic frameworks (Porter, BCG, perceptual maps)
- **Weak on:** Digital-native competitive intelligence tools (SimilarWeb, SEMrush, Crunchbase); real-time competitive monitoring; predictive competitive analysis using ML
- **Best for:** Strategic planning sessions; pitch-deck competitive positioning; founder education on market dynamics; ProcessBay client engagements requiring market-entry analysis
- **Not authoritative for:** Technical intelligence gathering (OSINT, dark-web monitoring); antitrust or regulatory implications of competitive behavior; advanced game-theoretic competitive modeling
