# Customer Feedback Loop

## Definition

A customer feedback loop is a structured system for capturing customer signals — surveys, interviews, support interactions, behavioral data — analyzing them, and routing insights back into product, marketing, and operational decisions. The "loop" means the firm closes the cycle: customers see their input changing the experience. Pereira's primary example is Netflix, which gathers feedback through surveys and data analysis to understand what content customers want and how they want to consume it, then uses those insights to create personalized recommendations and refine the user interface — directly driving satisfaction and retention.

## Key attributes

- **Multi-channel capture** — surveys, interviews, support tickets, behavioral data, social listening
- **Structured analysis** — feedback is categorized, prioritized, and quantified, not just collected
- **Closed loop** — customers see their input acted on (or are told why not)
- **Routing discipline** — insights flow to the team that can act, not into a generic inbox
- **Cadence** — feedback is processed on a regular rhythm, not in sporadic bursts
- **Linked to drivers** — feedback themes connect back to identified value drivers

## When to use

- After product launch to detect early failure modes
- When NPS or CSAT stops moving — diagnose by deepening feedback capture
- For high-churn segments where the firm needs to understand why customers leave
- To validate product roadmap priorities (does the customer agree with our bets?)
- In B2B accounts where qualitative depth matters more than survey breadth

## When NOT to use

- As a substitute for product/strategy judgment — feedback informs, leaders decide
- When the firm doesn't have capacity to act on what feedback reveals (creates frustration)
- For directional questions that feedback can't answer (e.g., "should we pivot the business")
- If the team will collect feedback but not close the loop (worse than not collecting)

## Common pitfalls

- Collecting feedback without analyzing it (data graveyard)
- Acting on the loudest voices instead of the dominant signal
- Not closing the loop — customers tire of being asked and ignored
- Confusing customer-requested features with what they'll actually use
- Letting one bad NPS comment derail the strategy

## Key metrics

- Response rate (how many customers actually participate)
- Time to close the loop (signal → action → notification)
- % of roadmap items traceable to a feedback theme
- Feedback theme repeat rate (recurring complaints = unmet need)
- NPS / CSAT trend over time

## Decision questions

- What are our 3 most reliable customer feedback channels, and how often do we synthesize them?
- For each major customer complaint theme: are we addressing it, or accepting it?
- Do customers know we read their feedback? — how do we close the loop?
- Are we measuring feedback volume AND quality, or just volume?
- What feedback insight changed our roadmap last quarter?
