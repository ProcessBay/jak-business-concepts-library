# Partner Due Diligence

## Definition

Partner due diligence is the systematic methodology for evaluating a prospective strategic partner across financial, legal, operational, cultural, and strategic dimensions before committing to a partnership. Pereira positions it as a multi-layered investigation — far more rigorous than a vendor check — covering complementary strengths, cultural alignment, market-specific insights, financial stability (audited statements, cash-flow projections, capital structure), legal and regulatory compliance history (past disputes, IP issues), references and case studies (both successful and unsuccessful partnerships), and long-term vision. The methodology extends through three lenses: scouting (broad market scan), suitability evaluation (alignment assessment), and risk assessment (operational, financial, legal, external/geopolitical).

## Key attributes

- **Multi-dimensional scope** — financial, legal, operational, cultural, strategic, geopolitical
- **Tiered investigation** — surface scan → deep dive → external verification
- **Cultural-alignment focus** — interviews, site visits, past-actions analysis — not just brochure-reading
- **Financial-stability emphasis** — audited statements, cash flow, capital structure, ratings
- **Legal-and-compliance review** — past disputes, IP, regulatory violations, licensing
- **Reference triangulation** — both successful and failed partnerships interviewed
- **Long-term-vision check** — does the partner's 5-year strategy still want this partnership?
- **Risk-and-contingency planning** — what external shocks could derail this?

## When to use

- Before any equity-bearing partnership (JV, strategic investment, M&A)
- Before R&D alliances or co-innovation programs that share IP
- Before entering a new geographic market via a local partner
- Before any partnership where switching cost (post-commitment) would be high
- Before partnerships in regulated industries (healthcare, finance, defense) where compliance failure carries reputational risk

## When NOT to use

- For purely transactional supplier relationships where switching is costless
- For short-term co-marketing campaigns with no IP or capital exposure
- When the partner is publicly traded with extensive disclosure and a clean record (lighter version suffices)
- When the cost of DD would exceed the partnership's value at risk

## Common pitfalls

- Treating DD as a checkbox — superficial review surfaces no real risks
- Overweighting financial DD and underweighting cultural fit — culture causes most partnership failures
- Skipping reference calls to failed-partnership counterparties — that's where the truth lives
- Missing regulatory or IP red flags in cross-border partnerships
- Failing to involve operational leaders — DD becomes a legal exercise divorced from how the partnership will run
- Letting deal momentum override DD findings — discovered risks get rationalized away
- Skipping post-DD risk mitigation planning

## Key metrics

- Number and severity of DD findings raised
- Partnership failure rate (DD-screened vs. baseline)
- Post-close surprises uncovered (lower is better)
- Dispute rate during partnership lifecycle
- Regulatory violations caught vs. missed
- Partner NPS / relationship-health (post-DD predictive validity)

## Decision questions

- Did we actually verify financial stability, or just take their statements at face value?
- Have we interviewed previous failed partners — not just success stories?
- Does our DD team include operational leaders, not just legal and finance?
- What cultural or operational red flags would actually cause us to walk away?
- What's our risk-mitigation plan for the top three findings we surfaced?
- Are we anchored on the deal or willing to kill it if DD reveals a fatal flaw?

## Canonical examples

- **Pre-JV pharmaceutical alliance DD** — Audited financial statements, IP portfolio review, regulatory compliance history, clinical capability audit (pages 30, 42)
- **International market-entry partner DD** — Local market share, distribution networks, government and stakeholder relationships, geographic-specific compliance (pages 29-30)
- **Strategic investment DD** — Cap structure, IP, customer/supplier contracts, pending litigation; full pre-investment review (page 42)
