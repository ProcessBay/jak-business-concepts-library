# Marketing Channel Selection

## Definition

Marketing channel selection is the decision of where to reach the target audience and with what mix of mediums. Pereira documents thirteen channels that should be evaluated for inclusion in any marketing plan: social media, websites/blogs, email marketing, omnichannel, video, influencer, offline advertising, online advertising, SEO, podcast, word-of-mouth (WOMM), partnership marketing, and community building. The selection is not "pick one" — HubSpot predicted 81% of marketers would use more than three channels by 2022. The principle is to match channel to audience behavior: a channel successful for one organization may not be successful for another (Pereira's example: Company A uses HubSpot for sales, Company B uses Pinterest with a dedicated sales team). Channel selection must be done before budgeting and message development.

## Key attributes

- **Thirteen channels Pereira inventories** — social, web/blog, email, omnichannel, video, influencer, offline, online ads, SEO, podcast, WOMM, partnership, community
- **Omnichannel default** — 81% of marketers use 3+ channels; single-channel rarely sufficient
- **Audience-led** — channel selection follows from where the target persona lives
- **Channel ≠ universal** — what works for one organization may not for another
- **Online + offline blend** — 50%+ of the world had internet access by 2019; offline still reaches a sizable segment
- **Earned vs. paid vs. owned** — channels split into earned media (WOMM, community), paid (ads, influencer), owned (website, email)
- **B2B vs. B2C channel preference differs** — video and LinkedIn skew B2B; Instagram/TikTok skew B2C
- **Channel-specific metrics** — engagement on social ≠ open rate on email ≠ rank on SEO

## When to use

- When building the marketing plan — channel selection is a required input
- When current channels are underperforming and you need to diversify
- When entering a new segment whose channel behavior differs from existing customers
- When launching a new product and need to determine the launch-channel mix
- When auditing marketing spend — which channels are over- vs. under-funded vs. ROI

## When NOT to use

- Without a defined target audience — channel choice without persona is guessing
- Without budget context — every channel costs differently; channel + budget decisions are paired
- For a single tactical campaign — campaign brief, not full channel re-selection
- When the team lacks bandwidth to operate the chosen channels well — fewer channels operated well beats many done poorly

## Common pitfalls

- **Distribution-only thinking** — Pereira: choosing channels without crafting a differentiated message is a common failure mode
- **Single-channel dependency** — risk concentration; one platform change can wipe out reach
- **Ignoring offline** — assumption that all customers are online misses a meaningful segment
- **Influencer selection by follower count** — Pereira: engagement rate matters more
- **Spamming email lists** — "It would be best to avoid being labeled as spammer at all costs"
- **Inconsistent message across channels** — same ad description should be used so audiences don't confuse the company for another
- **No tracking infrastructure per channel** — can't attribute conversion if not measuring
- **Partnership marketing without due diligence** — picking partners whose audience doesn't overlap with target

## Key metrics

- **Per channel** — CAC, conversion rate, engagement rate, attributed revenue
- **Social** — followers, engagement rate (not just count), share of voice
- **Email** — open rate, click-through, list growth, unsubscribe rate
- **SEO** — keyword rankings, organic sessions, domain authority
- **Influencer** — engagement rate (Pereira: "look at the engagement rate instead of the follower counts"), influencer-attributed conversions
- **Online ads** — CPC, CPM, conversion rate, retargeting effectiveness
- **Podcast** — downloads, listen-through rate, sponsor attribution
- **Partnership** — lead source attribution; KPIs Pereira flags: "be sure you have a reliable system in place to identify the source of each lead and sale"
- **Community** — active members, posts per member, brand-mention sentiment

## Decision questions

- Which channels does our target persona actually use, and at what stages of the buyer journey?
- Are we covering the full funnel (awareness through retention), or only one stage?
- What is our mix of paid, earned, and owned channels?
- Are we measuring channel-level ROI, not just channel-level activity?
- Which channels carry concentration risk if the platform changes?
- For influencer/partnership — are we evaluating engagement quality, not just reach?
- For omnichannel — is the message consistent across channels?

## Canonical examples

- **Social media** — Pereira: 3.5B users in 2019; spans Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, Instagram, YouTube, Snapchat, Pinterest (page 75)
- **Email marketing** — "interact directly with customers who have already demonstrated interest"; permission is essential (page 77)
- **Omnichannel** — "HubSpot predicts that by 2022, 81% of marketers will use more than three channels" (page 77)
- **Video** — "marketers use video more than any other media type"; short-form content is king for B2B and B2C (page 78)
- **Influencer** — TopRank Marketing: "86% of business-to-business firms benefit from influencer marketing" (page 78)
- **Offline advertising** — billboards, business cards, mail, flyers/brochures; "more than half of the world's population had access to the internet at the end of 2019" — meaning a sizable segment still attracts via offline (page 79)
- **SEO** — keyword optimization, mobile compatibility, quality content (pages 80-81)
- **Podcast** — passively consumed; "excellent medium for reaching people on their way to work" (page 81)
- **Word-of-Mouth (WOMM)** — "happens when consumers talk positively about a company's goods or services to others" (page 82)
- **Partnership** — sponsorship, co-hosting events, joint products, referral, affiliate (page 82)
- **Community building** — "individuals who share a common affinity for your brand and want to interact with other people who share that interest" (page 83)
