# Sales Closing Techniques

## Definition

Closing is the set of techniques used to bring a qualified prospect to a signed agreement. Pereira reframes closing — instead of the classic "Always Be Closing" image of a high-pressure ask, the modern sales view treats closing as a series of micro-commitments (micro-sales) that compound through the funnel until the final agreement becomes the natural next step. The macro close is preceded by qualifying for fit, validating BANT/4-of-5, addressing concerns, and building rapport; if those steps are done well, the final ask is small and often anticipated by the buyer. Pereira documents the most common technique pairings — the now-or-never close ("I can give you X% off if you decide today"), the question close ("Does what I'm offering address your problem?"), the assumptive close, and the soft-ask. The biggest closing failure is not asking at all — reps secretly afraid of rejection delay the question until the deal goes cold.

## Key attributes

- **Series of micro-closes, not one macro-close** — every stage advancement is a small commitment
- **Ask explicitly** — not asking is the #1 closing failure (page 172)
- **Confidence, not pressure** — closing strategies should be crystal clear, brief, powerful
- **Multiple technique types** — now-or-never, question close, assumptive, soft-ask, takeaway
- **Timing matters** — too early kills, too late lets the deal go cold
- **No "golden moment"** — if you're debating whether to ask, it's already late
- **Pairs tightly with objection handling** — every closed-lost has an unhandled objection
- **Best preceded by full qualification** — closing a 4/5-qualified opp is straightforward

## When to use

- After completing discovery, qualification, pitch, and objection handling
- When the prospect has acknowledged the problem and validated the solution fit
- When momentum is building — strike before the deal cools
- During negotiation — closing techniques include price/term-anchored asks
- Anytime a natural next-step appears (sign, contract, kickoff date)

## When NOT to use

- Before completing qualification — closing too early signals desperation
- When unhandled objections still block the deal — close the objection first
- On borderline-fit prospects who shouldn't be customers (page 149 anti-pattern)
- When pressure tactics would damage the relationship (enterprise, long-term accounts)
- As a substitute for value delivery — no close technique saves a weak value proposition

## Common pitfalls

- Not asking at all — the most common close failure (page 172)
- Generic "checking in" follow-ups instead of substantive value + ask
- Forcing the close on borderline deals (page 149 — damages brand, drives churn)
- Confusing closing with pressure tactics (the high-pressure tactics list on page 143 is what to avoid)
- "Always Be Closing" misinterpreted as constant pressure rather than constant micro-commitment
- No "walk-away number" in negotiation — caves on price reflexively
- Discounting to close instead of demonstrating value
- One-and-done close attempts; most deals require 5+ touches (page 170)

## Key metrics

- **Win rate** — Closed-Won / Total Opportunities
- **Close cycle length** — days from Proposal to Close
- **Ask rate** — % of qualified opps where the rep actually asks for the sale
- **Discount frequency** — % of closes with discount; high frequency signals weak close skill
- **Deal velocity at close stage** — speed once in Close stage
- **Close rate by close technique** — A/B testing reveals which work for your motion

## Decision questions

- Are reps asking for the sale, or hoping the deal closes itself?
- Are we closing on value, or defaulting to discounts?
- Do reps have a defined walk-away point in negotiation, or do they cave?
- Are we tracking which close techniques actually work for our motion?
- Are we close-asking too early (pressure) or too late (deal cools)?
- Do reps know how to "ask for it" without sounding pushy or desperate?

## Canonical examples

- **The Now-or-Never Close** (page 80): "I can give you a __% discount if you subscribe right now"
- **The Question Close** (page 80): "Do you think what I am offering addresses your problem?"
- **The Assumptive Close** — proceed as if the decision is made ("When should we kick off?")
- **The Soft-Ask / Takeaway** — "Sounds like this might not be the right fit — should we pause?"
- **Pereira's "5+ touches to close" rule** (page 170) — most reps quit after 2; closers persist
- **Pereira's "ask is the natural next step"** (page 172) — when qualification is done, the ask is easy
