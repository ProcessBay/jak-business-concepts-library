# Five Steps to Design an Exponential Business Model

## Definition

This is Pereira's prescribed five-step process for designing a new exponential business from a blank page. It is written for two audiences simultaneously: the founder building a new ExO from scratch, and the intrapreneur attempting to incubate an exponential venture on the edge of an established organization. The author is explicit that retrofitting an existing business model is a trap — incumbent assets and organizational immune systems will distort the design. The process therefore begins with deliberate detachment from existing assets and proceeds through MTP definition, problem/solution fit, ExO Canvas design, and BMC alignment. The worked example used throughout the chapter is "What if Hilton tried to invent Airbnb?" — illustrating both the power of the process and the difficulty of doing it inside a legacy organization.

## Key attributes

- **Five sequential steps with iteration loops** — Start Fresh → Define MTP → Find Global Problem + Scalable Solution → Define ExO Canvas → Define BMC based on ExO Canvas (pages 79-83)
- **Detachment as step zero** — existing assets and culture must be set aside (page 80)
- **MTP comes before product** — the purpose is the design constraint, not the wrapper (page 81)
- **Global scope, technological solution** — solutions must be designed for worldwide reach using compound-tech leverage (page 82)
- **Two canvases in dialog** — the BMC and ExO Canvas iterate against each other (pages 83-85)
- **Edge-of-org placement for intrapreneurs** — new exponential models must be incubated outside the parent's operational and political reach (page 80)
- **Four-of-five attribute rule** — using at least four ExO attributes substantially raises odds of exponential growth (page 83)
- **Backtrack expected** — step 3 may fail; the author explicitly notes you should return and reformulate (page 82)

## When to use

- A founder is starting a new venture and wants to design for exponential growth from day one
- A corporate innovation team is launching an "edge" venture deliberately outside the parent BU
- An existing business is being reimagined from scratch (clean-sheet exercise) for strategic planning
- A team needs structured guidance to move from "we want to build something big" to a concrete design

## When NOT to use

- The venture is intrinsically linear (artisanal, regulated specialty, bespoke services) — the process will produce a bad design
- The team has no appetite to detach from existing assets and politics — go through the motions and you will produce theater
- The category genuinely lacks 10x technology leverage — better to acknowledge this and design a strong linear business
- You are mid-execution on a working model — the process is for design, not for in-flight pivots without strong cause

## Common pitfalls

- **Skipping Step 1 (Start Fresh)** — most failures originate here; teams quietly drag existing assumptions into the design
- **MTP as marketing copy** — defining the MTP as a tagline rather than a purpose powerful enough to attract talent and community (page 81)
- **Solving local problems** — the process is explicit that global problems are the substrate for exponential growth (page 82)
- **Treating Step 4 as a checklist** — ticking off all five ExO attributes without honest application
- **Skipping the BMC<->ExO oscillation** — one pass through each is not sufficient; iteration is the point (page 85)
- **Building inside the parent org without protection** — intrapreneurs who don't insulate the project from the corporate immune system get killed (page 80)

## Key metrics

- **MTP quality** — does it actually scare you (Richard Branson test, cited on page 81)?
- **Global problem-solution fit** — independent validation that the problem is global, not regional
- **SCALE attributes engaged** — count and depth of ExO Canvas blocks populated
- **BMC-ExO alignment score** — do the two canvases tell the same story?
- **Iteration cycles completed** — proxy for design rigor

## Decision questions

- Have we genuinely detached from existing assets, or are we still designing for the constraints we already know?
- Is our MTP big enough to attract the talent and community we will need?
- Is the problem we're solving global, and is the solution technologically scalable?
- For each ExO attribute, do we have a real plan or a placeholder?
- Have we run the BMC and ExO Canvas in dialog, or only side by side?
- If we're inside a parent org, have we secured the political insulation to actually execute?

## Canonical examples

- **Hilton-becomes-Airbnb worked example** — the chapter's running illustration: MTP ("everyone belongs everywhere"), problem (cold hotels, underused homes), solution (platform matching), ExO Canvas application (pages 80-83)
- **Airbnb's actual ExO Canvas** — referenced explicitly as the design exemplar (page 83)
- **General Electric's pivot** — applied lean methodology across core operations to enable exponential development (page 39)
