# Sales Onboarding Program

## Definition

A sales onboarding program is the structured first-30/60/90-day system that takes a new sales hire from offer-letter to fully productive quota-carrying rep. Pereira treats it as the single biggest determinant of long-term rep success — not just a checklist of paperwork and product slides, but a deliberately engineered sequence covering company immersion, role-specific training, mentorship pairing, interactive practice, ongoing feedback, technology fluency, cultural integration, explicit goal-setting, and post-onboarding support. The key insight: onboarding does not end after orientation week. The first 90 days set ramp velocity, and reps who don't reach productive milestones on schedule rarely catch up. World-class programs treat onboarding as the highest-leverage enablement investment because every week of ramp shaved off compounds across every future hire.

## Key attributes

- **Comprehensive orientation** — company history, mission, values, strategy before role specifics
- **Role-specific training modules** — product depth, target market, sales process, tools
- **Mentorship pairing** — every new hire matched to an experienced rep from week one
- **Interactive learning** — role-play, simulations, workshops; not just slides
- **Continuous feedback mechanisms** — weekly check-ins with manager AND mentor
- **Technology training** — CRM, sales engagement, intelligence tools — proficiency, not exposure
- **Cultural immersion** — team-building, cross-functional intros, belonging signals
- **Explicit goal-setting** — measurable 30/60/90-day milestones agreed upfront
- **Post-onboarding support** — refresh modules, advanced skill sessions after week 12
- **Access to learning hub** — centralized resources for self-directed continuation

## When to use

- Always — even one new hire deserves a structured program, not improvisation
- Especially when hiring more than 2-3 reps per quarter (informal doesn't scale)
- When sales motion is complex (long cycle, technical product, multi-stakeholder)
- After a major product launch or repositioning (existing reps also need re-onboarding)
- When ramp time exceeds benchmark for the segment (B2B SaaS healthy = 3-6 months)
- When 12-month retention of new hires is below 70%

## When NOT to use

- As substitute for hiring properly — onboarding amplifies talent, doesn't create it
- For founder-led sales motion with 1-2 reps (still needed, but informal is fine)
- When the product or ICP is unsettled — you'd be training reps on a moving target
- As a one-time event with no reinforcement (then it's just orientation, not onboarding)

## Common pitfalls

- Front-loading product knowledge while skipping discovery/objection skill development
- Pairing new hires with whoever's free, not the best-fit mentor
- No measurable milestones — "ramp when ramped" leads to soft accountability
- HR-led onboarding that never touches actual selling
- Death by deck — reps drown in content with no practice
- Skipping CRM/tools training, then blaming reps for poor data hygiene
- No post-90-day continuation, so skills decay
- Identical onboarding for AE, SDR, CSM, and partner roles
- Manager checks in only at 30/60/90 milestones, not weekly

## Key metrics

- **Time to first deal** — days from start to first booked revenue
- **Time to productivity** — until rep consistently produces at expected level
- **Ramp time** — until consistent quota attainment
- **90-day quota attainment** — % of new reps hitting milestone targets
- **Onboarding NPS** — rep satisfaction with the program (leading indicator)
- **12-month retention of new hires** — strong onboarding predicts retention
- **Time to CRM proficiency** — leading indicator for tool adoption

## Decision questions

- How long does our average new rep take to reach first deal and to quota?
- Do we have a written 30/60/90 plan with measurable milestones, or vague expectations?
- Does every new hire get a mentor on day one, or only when convenient?
- How much of week 1-4 is practice vs. lecture?
- Are managers running weekly 1:1s during onboarding, or going dark after orientation?
- Do we measure onboarding effectiveness — and adjust the program based on data?
- What happens at day 91 — continued development, or radio silence?
