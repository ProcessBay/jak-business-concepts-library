# EdTech Monetization Strategies (Pereira)

## Definition

Pereira's working frame for choosing how an EdTech business converts learners into revenue. Four main monetization strategies anchor the decision: (1) **Freemium / Free Trial** — basic features free, premium paid; (2) **Subscription** — weekly/monthly/annual recurring fee for full access; (3) **Advertisement** — ad-supported free tier, often with paid ad-removal; (4) **Marketplace** — instructors sell courses, platform takes a cut. Two cross-cutting GTM dimensions further shape the choice: **B2C vs B2B/institutional** (who is the buyer — learner, parent, school, or enterprise) and **consumer vs institutional approach** (whether the school is the customer or a free distribution channel). The decision frame is not "pick one" but "pick a primary and a secondary that fit your buyer, geography, product cadence, and growth stage." Pereira's four selection criteria — **customers you serve**, **where customers are based**, **product or service you're selling**, **business stage and growth plans** — operationalize the choice.

## Key attributes

- **Four primary strategies** — Freemium / Subscription / Ads / Marketplace
- **Two GTM dimensions** — B2C/B2B and consumer/institutional approach
- **Most EdTech businesses combine 2-3 strategies** — e.g., Duolingo runs ads + freemium subscription; Udemy runs marketplace + B2B enterprise
- **Buyer ≠ user** — distinctive to EdTech: students use, parents/schools pay, sometimes sponsors fund
- **Geography matters** — payment culture, sales-tax treatment, willingness to pay vary sharply by region
- **Stage-dependent** — growth-stage prefers freemium / PAYG (acquisition); mature-stage prefers subscription (retention)
- **Compounds with sub-vertical** — K-12 forces institutional; higher-ed/corporate-L&D enables marketplace and MOOC patterns

## When to use

- Choosing initial monetization model for a new EdTech build
- Repositioning an existing product whose current monetization isn't working
- Stress-testing investor pitch on revenue-model defensibility
- Designing a hybrid that combines two strategies (the most common real-world pattern)
- Comparing your model against competitive incumbents

## When NOT to use

- Pre-MVP — premature revenue-model lock-in before product-market fit
- When the answer is structurally forced (e.g., K-12 public school = institutional, full stop)
- As a substitute for primary research — this is a decision frame, not a market study

## Common pitfalls

- Treating the four strategies as mutually exclusive when most successful EdTech is hybrid
- Ignoring the buyer/user split — designing freemium that converts students who can't pay
- Forcing subscription on transactional purchase categories (one-off certification, exam prep)
- Defaulting to ads without honestly assessing the impact on a children's brand
- Picking marketplace without supply — most marketplace attempts die from cold-start supply
- Skipping the geography question — what works in the US often doesn't work in MENA or SE Asia

## Key metrics

- **Revenue mix by strategy** — % of revenue from each
- **Conversion rate per strategy** — free-to-paid, ad-impression-to-revenue, GMV-to-take
- **ARPU per strategy** — segment economics
- **Customer segment fit** — which strategy lands which buyer segment
- **Strategy concentration risk** — over-reliance on one revenue strategy

## Decision questions

- **Customer**: Who's the actual buyer — learner, parent, teacher, school admin, enterprise L&D?
- **Geography**: Will payment culture, regulation, and ad tolerance support our model in our target market?
- **Product cadence**: Is this used daily (subscription-friendly) or occasionally (transactional)?
- **Stage**: Are we acquiring (freemium / free trial) or retaining (subscription / enterprise contract)?
- **Hybrid**: What's the primary, what's the secondary, and what's the dependency between them?

## Canonical examples

- **Duolingo** — ad-supported free tier + freemium subscription (Super Duolingo); two strategies stacked
- **Udemy** — marketplace primary + B2B enterprise (Udemy Business) secondary
- **Coursera** — freemium / subscription / B2B enterprise / university-partnered degree (four stacked)
- **Khan Academy** — sponsored (no end-user monetization at all)
- **MasterClass** — subscription primary ($180/year annual)
- **Udacity** — paid nano-degrees + B2B enterprise (Udacity Enterprise)
- **2U** — pure B2B institutional (universities pay for online-degree-as-a-service platform)
- **Noon Academy (Saudi)** — freemium with paid premium tutoring (bottom-up + subscription)
- **Rwaq (Saudi)** — free MOOCs + paid certificates + B2B corporate training
