# Customer Journey Mapping

## Definition

Customer journey mapping is the practice of laying out every stage and touchpoint a customer experiences with a company — from initial awareness through post-purchase support and advocacy — and explicitly identifying friction, inconsistencies, and opportunities to improve at each step. In the flywheel context, Pereira treats it as the foundational diagnostic tactic: you cannot remove friction from the flywheel until you can see where customers actually experience it, and you cannot accelerate momentum until you've mapped both digital and physical interactions across the full lifecycle.

## Key attributes

- **End-to-end view** — covers awareness, consideration, purchase, onboarding, support, advocacy
- **Touchpoint-explicit** — every interaction (ad, email, support call, packaging) is named
- **Friction-revealing** — exposes handoffs, delays, and inconsistencies that the org rarely sees
- **Multi-channel** — combines digital and physical interactions in a single map
- **Customer-voiced** — built from real feedback, not internal assumptions
- **Iterative** — re-mapped on a cadence; not a one-time artifact
- **Cross-functional** — must be built and owned across marketing, sales, product, and CS

## When to use

- Designing or auditing a flywheel — mapping is step one before optimization
- Diagnosing why retention is dropping or NPS is declining without a clear cause
- Aligning sales, marketing, and CS around a shared view of the customer
- Before launching a new product or service so day-one CX is intentional
- After a merger or reorganization that disrupts customer-facing handoffs
- Onboarding new team members so they see the customer's experience, not just their function

## When NOT to use

- As a one-off slide for a leadership meeting (will become wallpaper)
- Without committing resources to act on the friction the map reveals
- In genuinely transactional categories with no real journey beyond purchase
- As a substitute for direct customer conversations (mapping supplements, doesn't replace)
- When the organization isn't aligned on who owns each touchpoint (map first, decide, then re-map)

## Common pitfalls

- Mapping the *ideal* journey instead of the *actual* one (sanitizes the friction away)
- Building it from inside-out (org chart) instead of outside-in (customer experience)
- Treating it as a marketing artifact and excluding product, support, and operations
- Mapping a generic "average customer" instead of segment-specific journeys
- Producing the map and never re-visiting it — journeys drift; maps must too
- Ignoring post-purchase stages (the flywheel's compounding lives in Delight)
- Skipping the metrics layer — a map without KPIs per stage can't be managed

## Key metrics

- **Per-stage conversion rate** — where do customers drop or stall?
- **First Response Time** and **Average Resolution Time** — friction at support touchpoints
- **CSAT per touchpoint** — granular satisfaction, not just overall
- **NPS** — the cross-journey health signal
- **Churn driver analysis** — which mapped friction points predict churn?
- **Time-to-value (TTV)** — how fast does the customer hit first meaningful outcome?
- **Repeat purchase rate / referral rate** — the journey's compounding output

## Pereira's five-step playbook (pages 21-23)

1. **Map the customer journey** — every stage, every touchpoint, digital and physical
2. **Focus on customer feedback** — surveys, social, reviews, direct interactions
3. **Identify areas for improvement** — find patterns; prioritize by impact × resources
4. **Implement changes** — update processes, invest in tools, refine messaging; align teams
5. **Measure success** — KPIs (CSAT, NPS, retention, response time, resolution time, conversion rate); iterate

## Decision questions

- Have we mapped the *actual* journey, or the one we wish customers experienced?
- Which touchpoint has the worst friction-to-impact ratio?
- Who owns each stage, and do they know it?
- Are we measuring per-stage metrics or only overall outcomes?
- How long since we last refreshed the map?
- Does our map include post-purchase / advocacy stages, or stop at the sale?
