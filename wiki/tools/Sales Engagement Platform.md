# Sales Engagement Platform

## Definition

A Sales Engagement Platform (SEP) is the rep-facing layer that orchestrates multi-touch, multi-channel outbound and follow-up sequences — emails, calls, LinkedIn messages, SMS — under a single workflow called a **cadence** (or **sequence**). Pereira positions SEPs as a distinct category from CRM and email marketing: they unify communication, track engagement at the touch level, and surface analytics on what's working per rep, per cadence, per persona. Where marketing automation runs brand-driven campaigns to thousands, SEPs run rep-driven plays to dozens or hundreds of named accounts, with each step either auto-sent or queued for the rep to personalize and send. They turn outbound from heroic individual effort into a measured, optimizable system.

## Key attributes

- **Cadence / sequence builder** — pre-defined ordered steps across email, call, social, SMS
- **Multi-channel orchestration** — one workflow spans every channel a rep uses
- **Auto vs. manual steps** — some touches sent automatically, others queued for rep review
- **Engagement tracking per touch** — opens, clicks, call connects, replies attributed to specific steps
- **Templates with personalization tokens** — rep-friendly templates merged with prospect data
- **A/B testing on cadences** — entire sequences and individual steps tested against each other
- **CRM-synced** — activity logged automatically; reps work in the SEP, data lives in CRM
- **Analytics by rep, cadence, and persona** — what works, for whom, by whom

## When to use

- Outbound-driven B2B sales with named-account or persona-based targeting
- SDR / BDR teams running standardized prospecting plays at volume
- Account Executive teams running structured follow-up after demos or proposals
- When inconsistency in rep follow-up is costing pipeline
- When you need data on which cadences and which touches actually drive replies and meetings
- Multi-channel motions (email + LinkedIn + phone) that need coordination

## When NOT to use

- Pure inbound motions where marketing automation handles nurture and reps just take calls
- Tiny sales teams where the tool's overhead exceeds its leverage
- Enterprise pursuit of single mega-deals where every touch is bespoke
- When reps will treat templates as final instead of personalizing — quality drops, deliverability craters
- When CRM hygiene is so bad that the SEP can't trust who's a duplicate or who's already in a sequence

## Common pitfalls

- Reps blasting the same template without personalization, damaging both reply rate and sender reputation
- Cadences too long — 15+ touches that annoy more than they engage
- Cadences too short — 2-3 touches and a prospect declared dead before they'd realistically respond
- No persona segmentation — same cadence for VP and IC, big-co and small-co
- Ignoring deliverability hygiene (warmup, sending limits, spam-trigger words)
- Measuring activity (touches sent) instead of outcome (replies, meetings, pipeline)
- Failing to retire stale cadences — old plays run forever despite degraded performance
- Treating it as a CRM replacement — it's a layer on top, not a substitute

## Key metrics

- **Cadence Completion Rate** — percentage of prospects who receive the full sequence
- **Reply Rate** — replies per prospects in cadence (1-5% strong for cold B2B)
- **Meetings Booked per Sequence** — conversion from outreach to qualified meeting
- **Touch-to-Reply Ratio** — touches required per reply
- **Rep Activity Volume** — emails sent, calls made, prospects added per rep per day
- **Conversion per Cadence** — pipeline generated per cadence variant

## Decision questions

- What's the right cadence length and channel mix for our personas and deal size?
- Which steps should be automated vs. require rep personalization?
- How do we measure cadence performance and retire underperformers?
- How do we keep reply rate up without burning out our domain reputation?
- Will reps actually use the platform, or will it become a second-system overhead?
- How does the SEP coexist with our marketing automation — who owns which contacts and when?

## Canonical examples

- **Outreach** — leading B2B sales engagement platform; cadences, AI insights, CRM sync
- **Salesloft** — SEP focused on rhythm, conversations, and revenue intelligence
- **Apollo.io** — combined SEP plus prospecting database
- **HubSpot Sales Sequences** — SEP functionality inside the HubSpot CRM
- **Salesforce Sales Engagement (formerly High Velocity Sales)** — native SEP layer on Salesforce (page 97)
