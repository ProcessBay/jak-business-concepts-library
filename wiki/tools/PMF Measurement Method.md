# PMF Measurement Method

## Definition

Pereira's three-step method for measuring product-market fit: **(1) research target customers**, **(2) use metrics and tracking to determine the market share you can capture**, and **(3) apply specific quantitative and qualitative PMF metrics** (pages 30-38). The method is deliberately mixed-mode — qualitative research at the top, market sizing in the middle, and a battery of quantitative + qualitative metrics at the bottom. The point is to triangulate: no single metric proves PMF, but the combination of structured customer interviews, market-share math, and the right basket of metrics (NPS, LTV/CAC, churn, retention, virality, AARRR, growth rate, market share) creates a defensible picture.

## Key attributes

- **Three sequenced steps** — research → market sizing → metrics (pages 30-37)
- **Step 1: research** via articles, social media, industry publications, and direct customer interviews (pages 30-31)
- **Step 2: market sizing** via TAM/SAM/SOM and SaaS Rule of 40 (pages 32-33)
- **Step 3: quantitative metrics** — NPS, LTV/CAC, churn rate, retention rate, virality, AARRR, growth rate, market share (pages 34-37)
- **Step 3 also includes qualitative feedback** — early adopters give the richest improvement signals (pages 37-38)
- **NPS calculation**: % promoters (9-10) minus % detractors (1-6) (page 34)
- **LTV/CAC target in SaaS**: 3:1 (page 35)
- **Pyramid alignment** — research conversations must surface misalignment between value-prop layers and customer perception (page 31)
- **Interview discipline** — go in with hypotheses about misalignment to be falsified (page 31)

## When to use

- Post-MVP launch when you need to know if you've hit PMF
- Quarterly PMF reviews as the company scales
- Pre-fundraising: assemble the measurement story before investor conversations
- Diagnosing why growth stalls
- Aligning a cross-functional team on what "PMF" means in practice

## When NOT to use

- Pre-product — you don't have data to feed the metrics yet
- As a one-time exercise — PMF can be lost; measurement must be ongoing
- Without the qualitative leg — quantitative alone misses the "why"
- When team can't act on the findings — measurement without action is theater

## Common pitfalls

- **Talking only to promoters** — Pereira explicitly warns to also include detractors and passive users (page 27 echoes here on page 31)
- **Skipping market sizing** — going to metrics without TAM/SAM/SOM math
- **Cherry-picking metrics** — choosing the ones that look best rather than the ones that signal fit
- **Vanity metrics** — high acquisition without activation (page 36)
- **Treating NPS as the only number** — NPS is useful but insufficient
- **Interview questions that don't surface misalignment** — soft questions yield soft data (page 31)
- **No common ground on which metrics matter** — Pereira: "find common ground on which metrics are important" (page 36)

## Key metrics

- **NPS** — promoter minus detractor % (page 34)
- **LTV/CAC** — target 3:1 in SaaS (page 35)
- **Churn Rate** — lower = better (page 35)
- **Retention Rate** — % of clientele retained over a period (page 35)
- **Virality** — referrals via WOM, measured via referral surveys (page 36)
- **AARRR** — acquisition, activation, retention, referral, revenue (page 36)
- **Growth Rate** — sustained growth signals durable PMF (page 37)
- **Market Share** — relative position vs competitors (page 37)
- **Rule of 40** — growth + profit ≥ 40% (page 33)
- **TAM/SAM/SOM** — market sizing (page 32)

## Decision questions

- Have we interviewed both promoters AND detractors? (page 27)
- Have we sized our market (TAM/SAM/SOM) and projected revenue?
- Do we hit the SaaS Rule of 40? If not, where's the gap? (page 33)
- What's our NPS, and what's it trending toward?
- Is our LTV/CAC at or above 3:1? (page 35)
- Are we measuring activation, or just acquisition? (page 36)
- Has the team aligned on which metrics actually define PMF for our product? (page 36)

## Canonical examples

- **Customer interview discipline** — surfacing misalignment in the value-prop pyramid via structured conversation (page 31)
- **NPS survey** — the workhorse PMF measurement tool (page 34)
- **Early-adopter feedback** — Pereira: early adopters are the most willing to give criticism (page 37)
- **Phone calls in early stage** — when client base is small, direct calls yield "invaluable information" (page 38)
- **Customer surveys at scale** — once client base grows, surveys replace 1:1 calls (page 38)
