# Cold Calling

## Definition

Cold calling is the tactic of making unsolicited phone calls to prospects who have not previously engaged with your brand, with the purpose of starting a sales conversation. Pereira's treatment is deliberately ambivalent — he both names cold calling as a still-useful outbound tactic ("cold calls aren't dead," page 54) and lists pure cold calling among sales tactics that "don't work anymore" (page 146). The reconciliation: blind, script-only cold calls are dead; research-driven warm calls are alive. The modern version requires preliminary research (CRM data, LinkedIn, recent triggers), a clear pre-call hypothesis about how you can help, and the discipline to introduce rather than pitch. Pereira cites that 75% of customers will do business with the first salesperson who shows up able to meet their needs — proximity wins, but only if the call is informed. Always pair with email, social touches, and live chat to soften the cold edge.

## Key attributes

- **Two flavors: cold and warm** — pure cold (dead) vs. research-informed warm (alive)
- **Pre-call research is non-negotiable** — Pereira: "Making a call without prior knowledge is futile"
- **Multi-channel layering** — best paired with email + social touches in the same cadence
- **Goal is introduction, not close** — "introduce yourself and establish rapport," not "complete a sale"
- **Scripts as guardrails, not handcuffs** — used to remember to listen, not to read
- **Call recording for coaching** — CRM-integrated recording is now standard
- **GDPR / regulatory compliance** — surveys, opt-in pathways, do-not-call lists matter
- **Speak clearly, confidently** — credibility falls fast when listeners strain to hear

## When to use

- Early-stage SaaS/B2B with no inbound flywheel (page 42)
- Named-account ABM motions where high-value prospects don't fill forms
- Following up on a trigger event (funding announcement, leadership change, web visit)
- Industries where phone is still the cultural norm (financial services, government, healthcare)
- After a warm intro to convert it into a meeting fast
- Re-engaging cold leads in CRM after a relevant company change (page 135)

## When NOT to use

- Without any preliminary research (Pereira: "futile")
- As the sole outbound channel (single-channel cold-call-only is the death spiral)
- For consumer SaaS or self-serve products with PLG motions
- In markets with strict outbound regulations and no opt-in pathway
- When the rep is unwilling to listen (cold calling rewards listening, not talking)

## Common pitfalls

- Reading a script verbatim instead of using it as a guardrail
- Speaking too fast or too quietly — credibility evaporates
- Pitching on the first call instead of introducing
- No follow-up plan after the call (most deals require 5+ touches)
- Hammering the prospect with too many calls and emails (page 154 — looks desperate)
- Giving up after 1-2 dials when data shows 5+ attempts is the norm
- No CRM logging — losing institutional memory of who said what
- Ignoring optimal call times (some industries answer mornings, others afternoons)

## Key metrics

- **Dials per day** — activity baseline (typical SDR: 50-100)
- **Connect rate** — answered / dialed (healthy: 10-20%)
- **Conversation rate** — meaningful conversations / connections
- **Meeting rate** — meetings booked / conversations (healthy: 20-30%)
- **Call-to-opportunity rate** — qualified opps / total calls
- **Avg attempts to connect** — usually 5-8 attempts per connect
- **Best-time-to-call** — by segment/persona

## Decision questions

- Are reps doing pre-call research, or just smiling-and-dialing?
- Is cold calling one channel in a multi-channel cadence, or our only outbound?
- Are we measuring connect rate AND conversation quality, or just dial volume?
- How many touch attempts before we mark a lead dead — and is that aligned to data?
- Are we recording and coaching calls, or letting reps practice on prospects?
- Are we calling at the right time of day for each segment?

## Canonical examples

- **Predictable Revenue / Aaron Ross / Salesforce SDR model** — modern cold-call-led SDR motion
- **ZoomInfo, Apollo, Outreach.io** — tech stack built around cold calling at scale
- **Cognism** (cited by Pereira) — research-driven outbound including cold calling
- **Financial services / wealth management** — still phone-heavy by industry norm
- **B2B SaaS SDR teams** — most still use cold calling as one of 3-4 channels
