# Acquisition Hacks (Pereira)

## Definition

Acquisition hacks are the set of low-cost, high-impact tactics that growth hackers use to bring new users into the funnel. Pereira organizes the discipline around five primary channels: social media, search engine optimization (SEO), paid advertising, influencer marketing, and content marketing. The common thread is precision targeting backed by analytics — every channel is instrumented, tested, and iterated. Acquisition hacks are not about spending more; they're about choosing the right channel for the audience, crafting messaging that converts, and feeding learnings back into the loop. The growth hacker's edge is treating each acquisition channel as an experiment portfolio rather than a fixed marketing plan.

## Key attributes

- **Five primary channels** — social media, SEO, paid ads, influencer marketing, content marketing
- **Audience-first** — channel choice driven by where the target audience already is
- **Platform-fit matters** — LinkedIn ≠ Instagram ≠ TikTok ≠ Google Ads
- **Always instrumented** — every channel tracked with conversion-tied KPIs
- **Test, kill, scale** — losing channels get cut; winning channels get more budget
- **Content as the asset** — high-quality content amplifies every other channel
- **Cost-per-acquisition discipline** — every channel justified by CAC vs LTV

## When to use

- Any stage of growth (founding through scale)
- When you need to diversify off a single dominant channel (channel risk)
- When acquisition cost is rising on existing channels
- When entering a new market or audience segment
- When competitors have a channel mix you have not tested

## When NOT to use

- Before product-market fit (acquisition amplifies churn)
- When the team lacks attribution to know which channel actually drove the customer
- When the brand cannot tolerate the experimentation messaging required for testing
- For ultra-niche B2B where direct sales outperforms any digital channel
- When unit economics don't support paid acquisition at any CAC

## Common pitfalls

- Spreading thin across all channels instead of mastering one or two
- Copying a competitor's channel mix without understanding their unit economics
- Treating influencer marketing as a fee-for-post transaction rather than authentic partnership
- Ignoring SEO compounding (a one-quarter horizon kills SEO investment)
- Confusing reach with acquisition (a million views, zero conversions)
- Failing to integrate channels (paid ads to a landing page that breaks email capture)

## Key metrics

- **CAC** (Customer Acquisition Cost) — by channel
- **Conversion rate** — visitor → lead → customer
- **CTR** (Click-Through Rate) — for paid and email
- **Cost per acquisition (CPA)** — paid-ad-specific
- **Return on ad spend (ROAS)**
- **Channel ROI** — full-loop revenue vs cost
- **Organic vs paid mix** — channel diversification

## Decision questions

- Where does our target audience actually spend their attention?
- Which one channel can we be best-in-class at, before adding more?
- What is our CAC by channel, and how does each compare to LTV?
- Are we measuring full-funnel conversion, not just top-of-funnel reach?
- Which channel compounds (SEO, content, community) vs which is rented (paid ads)?
