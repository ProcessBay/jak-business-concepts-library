# Email Marketing for Retention

## Definition

Email marketing for retention is the use of segmented, personalized, lifecycle-triggered email communication to keep customers engaged, drive repeat purchases, prevent churn, and deepen brand relationship over time. Pereira positions email as the most durable retention channel because it is owned media (not rented from a platform algorithm), trackable end-to-end, and infinitely segmentable. The discipline has nine pillars: list quality, segmentation, welcome flows, valuable content, personalized product recommendations, exclusive offers, drip automation, surveys/feedback, and lifecycle milestones. The growth hacker's edge in email is the ratio of work per send — well-built automation runs forever, and a marginal subscriber added today compounds for years.

## Key attributes

- **Owned channel** — not subject to platform algorithm changes or paid bidding
- **Segmentable infinitely** — by demographics, behavior, lifecycle stage, purchase history
- **Lifecycle-driven** — welcome, onboarding, engagement, win-back, milestone, churn-prevention
- **Automation-leveraged** — drip campaigns run continuously without per-send labor
- **Personalization-sensitive** — generic blasts underperform personalized sends by 5-10x
- **Frequency-disciplined** — over-sending kills the list faster than under-sending
- **Measurable end-to-end** — open, click, conversion, unsubscribe, revenue per send

## When to use

- Any business with a returning-customer model (SaaS, subscription, e-commerce repeat)
- Onboarding new users through their first weeks
- Re-engaging dormant users before they churn
- Lifecycle milestones (birthday, anniversary, usage milestones)
- Promoting new features, content, or offers to interested segments
- Following up on abandoned carts or incomplete sign-ups

## When NOT to use

- One-time-purchase categories with no retention upside
- Markets with strict opt-in rules where list-building is expensive (GDPR, CASL violations)
- When deliverability is broken (sender reputation, spam-folder placement)
- When the team can't produce content cadence that subscribers value
- When segmentation infrastructure doesn't exist (one-size-fits-all blasts)

## Common pitfalls

- Buying lists (kills deliverability, violates regulations)
- Sending the same message to everyone (low engagement, high unsubscribe)
- Over-sending (frequency erodes engagement and brand trust)
- Generic welcome emails that don't set expectations or deliver value
- Ignoring deliverability (no SPF/DKIM/DMARC, poor sender reputation)
- Treating email as broadcast instead of conversation (no reply path)
- Measuring opens only (open rates have been inflated since iOS 15)

## Key metrics

- **Open rate** (caveat: less reliable post-iOS Mail Privacy)
- **Click-through rate** (CTR)
- **Conversion rate** — sends that drove the target action
- **Unsubscribe rate** (and complaint rate)
- **Revenue per subscriber** (per month)
- **List growth rate** (net of unsubscribes)
- **Deliverability / inbox placement rate**

## Decision questions

- What is the lifecycle stage of each subscriber, and are we sending the right message for that stage?
- What value does each email deliver — and would the subscriber miss it if we stopped?
- Is our frequency calibrated for engagement, not vanity volume?
- Are we tracking revenue per send, or just opens and clicks?
- What's our automation coverage — what % of sends are triggered vs manually scheduled?

## Canonical examples

- **Amazon** — abandoned-cart, recommendation, post-purchase, review-request lifecycle automation
- **Spotify** — Discover Weekly email + year-end Wrapped campaign (page 104-105)
- **Duolingo** — daily streak reminders, lapsed-user win-back emails
- **Airbnb** — destination recommendations, trip-anniversary emails (page 97-98)
- **Substack / Patreon** — content-driven retention via writer/creator emails
- **Headspace / Calm** — meditation streak and milestone email cadence
