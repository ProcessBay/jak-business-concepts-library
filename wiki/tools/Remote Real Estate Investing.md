# Remote Real Estate Investing

## Definition

Remote real-estate investing (sometimes called virtual or online real-estate investing) is the practice of acquiring and operating investment property in a market where the investor does not physically live, using digital platforms, third-party teams, and remote-due-diligence tools in place of in-person presence. Pereira reports an 8%+ five-year increase in out-of-state real-estate investors, driven by (a) high prices and anti-landlord regulation in coastal US markets pushing investors to seek yield elsewhere, (b) the maturation of online listing, valuation, e-signing, and remote-tour technology, and (c) the rise of dedicated remote-investing platforms like Roofstock, FlipScout, Fundrise, and Airbnb. The thesis: by removing geographic constraints, an investor can target the best risk-adjusted yield nationally rather than settling for the local market. The tradeoff: thinner local knowledge, dependence on remote teams, and slower response to operational issues.

## Key attributes

- **Geography-agnostic sourcing** — invest where the math works, not where you live
- **Tech-stack dependent** — listing portals, 3D tours, drone footage, e-sign, remote notarization
- **Remote team required** — local property manager, contractor, agent, inspector
- **Higher information asymmetry** — local investors know things you don't
- **Often paired with buy-and-hold or BRRRR** — not typically used for active flipping
- **Diversification benefit** — spreads risk across multiple metro markets
- **Lower CAC for platforms** — fueled rise of Roofstock, Fundrise, Sundae, Mynd

## When to use

- Local market has unattractive yields, high prices, or hostile regulation
- Investor has capital but local opportunity is constrained
- Investor wants geographic diversification across multiple metros
- Remote tools (Matterport, Dealcheck, Roofstock listings) cover the diligence gaps
- Investor can build / hire a reliable local team in target market

## When NOT to use

- Investor relies on hands-on operations / rehab supervision
- Target market lacks reliable property-management infrastructure
- Investor has analysis-paralysis tendencies — remote requires more research, not less
- Capital insufficient to absorb mistakes from imperfect local information
- Asset class requires high-touch service (e.g., short-term rentals in some markets)

## Common pitfalls

- **Bad property manager = bad outcome** — entire investment depends on this hire
- **Trusting Zestimate or algorithmic comps** — always validate locally
- **Skipping in-person scouting trip** — at least once is worth the airfare
- **Late-night maintenance calls** — without a local fixer, small issues escalate
- **Tax / regulation gaps** — out-of-state investor often misses local nuance
- **Contractor / agent collusion** — remote investors are vulnerable to skimming
- **Underestimating turnover cost** — vacancy + leasing takes longer when remote

## Key metrics

- **Cap rate** — should be materially higher than home-market to justify the friction
- **Cash-on-cash return** — including remote management premium
- **Property management cost** — typically 8–12% of rent + leasing fees
- **Vacancy rate** — remote markets may run higher; budget conservatively
- **Cycle time on issues** — how long from problem reported to problem resolved
- **Travel + due-diligence cost** — should be amortized into deal underwriting

## Decision questions

- Is the cap-rate spread between my home market and target market wide enough to justify the friction?
- Do I have a property manager I trust — or a referral chain to one?
- What's my plan for the first time something goes wrong at 2am?
- Have I visited the market in person at least once before deploying capital?
- How am I validating remote comps — Zestimate, agent BPO, in-person appraisal?
- What's my exit option if the market or PM relationship deteriorates?

## Canonical examples

- **Roofstock** — marketplace for single-family rental properties; vetted listings, market rent and yield estimates; portfolio acquisition possible (page 92-93)
- **FlipScout** — AI / big-data platform for screening rentals and flips remotely (page 92)
- **Fundrise** — eREIT marketplace; remote investing with $500 minimums into diversified property portfolios (page 93)
- **Matterport** — 3D property tours; standard for remote diligence (page 93)
- **Dealcheck** — property analysis app for remote investors (page 94)
- **Airbnb** — operating remote short-term rentals (page 94)
- **Saudi/MENA**: remote investing is nascent in KSA. Inter-emirate / inter-city remote investing (Riyadh-based investor buying in Jeddah or Eastern Province) is emerging via **Bayut / Property Finder** listings. Cross-border GCC remote investment (Saudi → Dubai or vice versa) is more developed. **Sakani** and **Ejar** could become the diligence + operating spine for a true Saudi remote-investing stack as data transparency improves
