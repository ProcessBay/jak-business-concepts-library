# Sales Enablement Readiness Assessment

## Definition

A sales enablement readiness assessment is the structured pre-strategy audit that diagnoses an organization's current enablement state across seven dimensions before building or refreshing the program. Pereira positions it as the mandatory first step — building strategy without baseline is "designing in a vacuum" and produces enablement plans that look impressive but don't fit the actual gaps. The seven dimensions: (1) stakeholder alignment — do sales, marketing, leadership share understanding of goals; (2) content analysis — what exists, what's used, what's missing; (3) technology infrastructure — CRM, automation, SEP integration completeness; (4) skill and knowledge — where are the team's actual gaps; (5) performance metrics and analytics — what's tracked and how reliable; (6) customer feedback — what does buyer interaction reveal; (7) competitive landscape — how do peers do enablement. The output is a baselined gap analysis that defines the strategy that follows.

## Key attributes

- **Seven-dimension framework** — stakeholder alignment, content, tech, skills, metrics, customer feedback, competitive landscape
- **Baseline before action** — establishes starting point for delta measurement
- **Cross-functional input** — sales, marketing, leadership, frontline reps all contribute
- **Quantitative + qualitative** — metrics for what exists; interviews for why
- **Gap-focused output** — prioritized list of gaps with severity and impact
- **Drives downstream strategy** — assessment output is the strategy's foundation
- **Repeated periodically** — annual minimum, after major transitions
- **Honest about what's broken** — sugarcoated audits produce sugarcoated strategies

## When to use

- Before launching any new sales enablement program or major refresh
- When new sales leadership arrives and wants to baseline current state
- After major organizational change (merger, restructure, pivot)
- When existing enablement is underperforming and the root cause is unclear
- Annually as a discipline — assumptions drift even when nothing visibly changes
- Before requesting enablement headcount, tooling, or budget increase
- When sales-marketing alignment has visibly degraded

## When NOT to use

- As substitute for action — assessment paralysis is a real failure mode
- When the org won't act on findings (then it's just a report nobody reads)
- For tactical adjustments where 7-dimension audit is overkill
- Right after a previous assessment unless something major has changed
- As performance-management cover (audit ≠ blame)

## Common pitfalls

- Conducting the audit but not acting on findings
- Skipping frontline rep input — leaders self-report what they wish were true
- Quantitative-only — misses the why behind the numbers
- No baseline KPI capture — can't measure delta later
- Cherry-picking dimensions instead of all seven
- Single-stakeholder audit (just sales, or just marketing)
- Treating the report as the deliverable rather than the strategy it should drive
- Audit fatigue — repeating annually with no visible action erodes trust
- No prioritization — long laundry list with no severity ranking

## Key metrics

- **Readiness score per dimension** — 1-5 scale across the seven dimensions
- **Gap count by severity** — critical, high, medium, low
- **Baseline KPIs captured** — TTP, win rate, quota attainment, content utilization, etc.
- **Stakeholder alignment score** — quantified from interviews/surveys
- **Tech-stack integration completeness** — % of tools bidirectionally synced
- **Content-utilization baseline** — % of library deployed in last 90 days
- **Time to complete assessment** — typically 4-8 weeks for thorough audit

## Decision questions

- Have we measured baseline before starting, or are we designing blind?
- Have we audited all seven dimensions, or cherry-picked the comfortable ones?
- Have frontline reps contributed input, or only leadership?
- Do we have ranked, prioritized gaps — or an undifferentiated list?
- Are we prepared to act on findings, or is this an audit-to-the-shelf exercise?
- Who owns each gap, and what's the timeline to address?
- When will we re-assess to measure delta?
