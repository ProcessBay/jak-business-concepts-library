# Product Launch Playbook

## Definition

The Product Launch Playbook is Pereira's three-phase framework for executing a launch as a sustained motion, not a single-day event. **Phase 1 (Pre-Launch)** builds anticipation through market research, value proposition refinement, teaser campaigns, and assembling a cross-functional launch team. **Phase 2 (Launch Day)** maximizes visibility through coordinated events, multi-channel marketing, limited-time offers, and content rollouts. **Phase 3 (Post-Launch)** captures and compounds value through customer onboarding, feedback gathering, ongoing engagement, and upsell/cross-sell motion. The discipline's central insight: most products fail not at launch but in Phase 3 from neglect — launch day is the beginning of the work, not the end.

## Key attributes

- **Three sequential phases** — pre-launch, launch day, post-launch
- **Cross-functional launch team** — marketing, sales, product, support with defined roles
- **Pre-launch builds anticipation** — teasers, waitlists, hint content over weeks/months
- **Launch day is multi-channel** — coordinated across social, email, content, influencer, paid, PR
- **Limited-time offers manufacture urgency** — early adopter pricing, launch bundles, time-bound bonuses
- **Post-launch is where retention is won or lost** — onboarding, support, engagement
- **Feedback loop is structured** — surveys, reviews, direct outreach, not passive
- **A/B testing post-launch** — messaging, visuals, CTAs continuously refined
- **Post-launch surfaces the next wave** — upsell, cross-sell, expansion revenue

## When to use

- New product or major version launches
- Geographic or segment expansion of existing products
- Brand or positioning changes that require relaunch energy
- Annual flagship moments (Apple event, Dreamforce, F8) where ecosystem attention is concentrated
- Whenever you need cross-functional coordination on a time-bound objective

## When NOT to use

- For minor releases that don't warrant launch energy (point releases, feature flags)
- When the product isn't actually ready — launch playbook can't fix a broken product
- For continuous-deployment SaaS where launches are by definition incremental
- When marketing capacity is consumed by other priorities (mediocre launches dilute brand)

## Common pitfalls

- Treating launch day as the finish line — neglecting Phase 3 kills the launch's value
- Pre-launch teaser fatigue — too many vague hints over too long a period
- Launch day chaos — no clear ownership, last-minute coordination breakdowns
- Underestimating post-launch support load — Day 1 issues swamp an unprepared team
- No measurement plan — can't tell after if the launch worked
- Channel silos — sales, marketing, and product running parallel launches with different messages
- Failing to capture launch-day testimonials and content for the long tail
- Promising "more coming soon" that never comes — erodes future launch credibility

## Key metrics

- **Pre-launch waitlist / sign-ups** — leading indicator of demand
- **Launch day sign-ups / purchases / activations** — the moment-of-truth metric
- **Week 1 activation rate** — % of launch buyers who actually use the product
- **Day 30 retention** — early-cohort retention curve
- **Social mentions / share of voice during launch week**
- **Press / earned media coverage** — number and quality of placements
- **Cost per acquisition during launch window** — compare to steady-state CAC
- **NPS at Day 30** — early product-market resonance signal

## Decision questions

- Do we have a cross-functional launch team with clear owners for each phase?
- What's our launch's narrative — and can every channel deliver it consistently?
- How long should pre-launch run — long enough to build anticipation, short enough to avoid fatigue?
- What limited-time offer makes sense without anchoring our pricing low forever?
- How will we handle the Day 1 / Week 1 support surge?
- What feedback channels are open from Day 1, and who reviews the feedback?
- What's the Phase 3 plan — and is it resourced as seriously as Phase 1 and 2?

## Canonical examples

- **Apple product launches** — multi-month pre-launch teasers, live keynote launch day, sustained post-launch retail and content (page 115)
- **Tesla Roadster / Model S launches** — direct-to-consumer reservations as pre-launch, dramatic reveals, OTA-driven post-launch iteration (page 127-128)
- **SaaS product launches** — Product Hunt + beta waitlist + launch week content + customer success motion
- **Gaming launches** — pre-orders, launch day events, day-one patches as post-launch
- **Consumer goods (e.g., Liquid Death, Athletic Greens)** — influencer-seeded pre-launch, coordinated launch week, sustained content engine post-launch
