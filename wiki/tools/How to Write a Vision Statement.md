# How to Write a Vision Statement

## Definition

Pereira documents a six-stage development process for crafting a compelling vision statement: (1) Evaluate the current state — values, mission, strategic position, industry context; (2) Involve stakeholders — internal (executives, employees) and external (customers, partners, community); (3) Conduct market research — segments, trends, opportunities; (4) Seek professional facilitation when politics or bias risks distorting the process; (5) Treat it as iterative — multiple drafts, reviews, continuous improvement; (6) Use structured brainstorming techniques to generate raw material. The output is a vision statement that is owned (not just announced), tested (not just written), and operational (not just inspirational). This is the *craft process* — the underlying anatomy of what a vision *is* lives in [[Vision Statement]].

## Key attributes

- **Six-stage process** — Evaluate → Involve → Research → Facilitate → Iterate → Brainstorm
- **Stakeholder-inclusive** — internal and external voices throughout, not just at launch
- **Evidence-based** — grounded in market research, not pure leadership imagination
- **Iterative** — multiple drafts, reviewed against drafts, refined against feedback
- **Facilitated when needed** — neutral third party breaks deadlock, surfaces blind spots
- **Brainstorming-driven** — uses 7 named techniques to generate raw input
- **Anchored in current-state assessment** — you can only chart a course from a known starting point

## When to use

- New company foundation
- Post-merger / post-acquisition vision alignment
- Strategic pivot after major market shift
- New CEO / leadership transition
- When the existing vision no longer reflects what the firm is becoming
- Before a Cascade / OKR / Hoshin Kanri rollout (vision is the apex layer)

## When NOT to use

- When leadership intends to ignore the output (process erodes trust)
- During acute operational crisis (defer; vision work needs reflective bandwidth)
- As a substitute for strategy — vision precedes strategy, not replaces it
- When the firm hasn't decided who its stakeholders are yet
- Annually as ritual (vision is timeless; revisit every 3-5 years)

## Common pitfalls

- Skipping current-state evaluation and jumping straight to aspiration
- Drafting in a closed leadership room and announcing top-down
- Stakeholder consultation as theater (collecting input, ignoring it)
- Treating it as a one-and-done writing exercise rather than iterative
- Letting internal politics shape it (use a facilitator to neutralize bias)
- Confusing brainstorming output with the final statement
- No mechanism for how the vision will be operationalized after launch
- Ignoring resistance to change (employees who liked the old direction)

## Key metrics

- **Stakeholder participation rate** — % of identified groups consulted
- **Draft iteration count** — typically 3-7 rounds before consensus
- **Employee buy-in score** — survey after launch: "Do you feel ownership of this vision?"
- **Vision recall rate post-launch** — can people state it accurately at 30/90 days?
- **Time to first draft** — usually 4-12 weeks for a serious process
- **Cross-functional representation** — number of departments/levels involved

## Decision questions

- Have we honestly evaluated where we are today before chasing where we want to be?
- Which stakeholder groups are missing from the conversation?
- Are we writing what we believe, or what looks good in a deck?
- Who facilitates when partners/leaders disagree?
- How many drafts have we shipped to test — and how did we revise based on feedback?
- What's our plan for operationalizing this in the first 90 days?

## Canonical examples

- **Saudi Vision 2030 process** — Crown Prince mandate followed by Council of Economic and Development Affairs (CEDA) consultation, McKinsey/Boston Consulting facilitation, sector-by-sector stakeholder workshops, iteration over months before the April 2016 launch. Real-world demonstration of the full Pereira stack: evaluate (KSA reliance on oil), involve (ministries, private sector, citizens via consultation), research (sector baselines), facilitate (external consultants), iterate (revised KPIs at 2020 mid-point), brainstorm (vision realization programs). (page 48-62 process; Vision 2030 is the canonical national-scale execution)
- **Microsoft's vision refresh under Nadella** — current-state evaluation honestly named cultural problems before any forward statement was drafted (pages 48-49 — evaluate current state)
- **Vision workshops at scale** — Pereira recommends town halls, focus groups, surveys, individual interviews tailored to stakeholder role (page 50)
- **The 7 brainstorming techniques** — Mind Mapping, SWOT, Scenarios & Future Prediction, Analogies & Metaphors, Cross-functional Collaboration, Lateral Thinking (de Bono), Storytelling (pages 59-62)
