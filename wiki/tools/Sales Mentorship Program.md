# Sales Mentorship Program

## Definition

A sales mentorship program structures the pairing of experienced reps with less-tenured colleagues to transfer tacit knowledge, accelerate skill acquisition, and build cohesion — distinct from manager coaching (which is hierarchical and tied to performance management). Pereira frames mentorship plus peer-to-peer learning as the third leg of the development stool alongside formal training and manager coaching: they reach what classroom content can't (deal nuance, customer politics, internal navigation) and at lower cost than 1:1 coaching. Effective programs are structured (not "find someone you like"), aligned to business objectives, supported by mentor training, and goal-oriented with explicit expectations on both sides. Peer-to-peer extensions include best-practice circles, brown-bag sessions, and online forums that turn the team's collective experience into a continuously-refreshed knowledge base.

## Key attributes

- **Structured pairings** — defined mentor-mentee matches with framework, not ad-hoc
- **Aligned to business objectives** — mentorship supports specific company goals
- **Mentor selection and training** — not every senior rep is a good mentor; train them
- **Goal-oriented relationships** — explicit objectives set at the start, reviewed quarterly
- **Career development focus** — beyond immediate skill, toward long-term growth
- **Personalized guidance** — tailored to mentee's strengths, weaknesses, aspirations
- **Knowledge-sharing culture** — mentorship creates a ripple effect across the team
- **Community building** — multiple mentor-mentee pairs create network effects
- **Complements coaching** — peer wisdom adds to manager-led development

## When to use

- Always pair every new hire with a mentor on day one of onboarding
- When the team has 10+ reps and tacit knowledge needs deliberate transfer
- During scaling — formal mentorship multiplies senior-rep impact
- When DEI is a priority — mentorship widens access to advancement
- For specific transitions (SDR to AE, AE to senior AE, IC to manager)
- When peer-to-peer learning would unlock collective expertise (best-practice circles)

## When NOT to use

- As substitute for manager coaching — they serve different purposes
- When senior reps refuse to mentor (forced mentorship is poison)
- Without mentor training (good reps can be bad mentors)
- With no structure or accountability (becomes a calendar invite no one attends)
- When the team is too small (1-3 reps) for meaningful pairing

## Common pitfalls

- Pairing by convenience instead of fit (similar style, complementary strengths)
- No mentor training — sink-or-swim approach burns good senior reps
- No defined goals — mentor and mentee don't know what success looks like
- No protected time — meetings get pre-empted by deal work
- Mentorship treated as charity, not as career-relevant for mentors
- One-time pairing with no re-matching when needs change
- Confusing mentorship with shadowing — passive vs. active
- Ignoring peer-to-peer learning beyond formal pairs (brown bags, circles, forums)
- No recognition for mentor contribution

## Key metrics

- **Mentee ramp acceleration** — do mentored reps reach productivity faster than unmentored?
- **Mentor NPS / mentee NPS** — both sides rating the experience
- **Knowledge transfer score** — pre/post assessment on mentor-taught topics
- **Mentee 12-month retention** — mentored reps stay longer (well-documented)
- **Peer-learning participation** — attendance at brown-bag and best-practice circles
- **Mentor pipeline health** — senior reps' own performance during mentorship period
- **Internal promotion rate** — mentored vs. unmentored reps promoted

## Decision questions

- Does every new hire get a mentor on day one, or only when the planets align?
- Are mentors trained, or did we just ask the top rep to "help out"?
- Do mentor-mentee pairs have explicit goals and a meeting cadence?
- How are we measuring mentorship impact — beyond mentor enthusiasm?
- Is there a peer-learning forum (brown bag, circles) beyond 1:1 pairs?
- Do we recognize and reward mentor contribution in performance reviews?
- When should pairs be re-matched, and who decides?
