# Guided Customer Onboarding

## Definition

Guided Customer Onboarding is the deliberate, structured process of moving a new customer from sign-up to first value — closing the gap between purchase and productive use. Pereira calls it "the most important part of every business-to-business organization" because even the most carefully constructed customer success strategies fail when onboarding is left to chance (page 36). It blends welcome communications, sequential setup tutorials, knowledge-base self-serve content, in-product nudges (banners, pop-ups), and direct human help from CSMs or sales reps. The bar is straightforward: by the end of onboarding, the customer can use the product to accomplish what they bought it for — and feels confident doing so without help. Pereira frames it as the bridge between Acquisition (Pillar 3) and Value Realization (Pillar 6) in the eight-pillar strategy.

## Key attributes

- **Welcome contact is non-negotiable** — first email goes out immediately after sign-up (page 45)
- **Follow-up cadence** — automated reminders for customers who stall mid-setup (page 46)
- **Self-serve + assisted blend** — knowledge base for autonomy, human help for complex needs
- **In-product guidance** — banners, pop-ups, and automated messages surface features at the right moment (page 46)
- **Templates at scale** — pre-built email sequences and tutorials for common segments (HelpCrunch model)
- **Sequential tutorials** — clear, ordered steps rather than dumping the full product on the user
- **Owned by CS, not Sales** — handoff from Sales to CSM is the riskiest moment in the lifecycle
- **First-impression weight** — customer perception of the brand is formed here (page 53)

## When to use

- Any subscription product where adoption gaps cause silent churn
- Complex B2B tools where the customer can't self-discover value within a trial window
- After major product updates that change core workflows
- When data shows time-to-value is too long or activation rates are flat
- Free trial or freemium acquisition motions where conversion depends on activation
- Enterprise deployments where multiple stakeholders need different onboarding tracks

## When NOT to use

- Products simple enough that any onboarding gets in the user's way (over-engineered for the use case)
- Highly bespoke services where every engagement is custom (use kick-off workshops instead)
- Very low-ticket products where the unit economics can't sustain assisted onboarding
- When the real problem is product, not onboarding — onboarding can't rescue a bad PMF

## Common pitfalls

- Treating onboarding as a single welcome email instead of a multi-step program
- No follow-up for customers who stall mid-setup (page 46)
- Front-loading the entire feature set instead of sequencing to first value
- One template for all segments — enterprise and SMB need different paths
- Sales over-promises, then CSM owns the disappointment
- No measurement of where customers drop off in the onboarding flow
- Confusing "completed onboarding" with "achieved value" — different metrics
- Hand-offs from Sales without context transfer

## Key metrics

- **Time-to-Value (TTV)** — days from sign-up to first value moment
- **Onboarding Completion Rate** — % of new customers who finish the program
- **Activation Rate** — % who hit the defined activation milestone
- **Early Churn Rate** — % churning within first 30/60/90 days
- **Onboarding NPS** — survey at end of onboarding
- **Drop-off Funnel** — where customers stall in the sequence

## Decision questions

- What's our defined "first value" milestone — and how long does it take today?
- Where do customers drop off in the current flow?
- Do we have segment-specific paths, or one size for all?
- Is the Sales-to-CSM handoff documented and clean?
- Do we measure onboarding by completion (activity) or by value reached (outcome)?

## Canonical examples

- **HelpCrunch** — welcome email immediately after sign-up, friendly follow-ups for stalled installs, template email library, knowledge base with "How to set up an account?" guides, banner/pop-up nudges, dedicated "customer success heroes" for emergency and preventative help (pages 45-46)
- **HubSpot** — HubSpot Academy classes paired with knowledge base, onboarding paths by product tier and customer size (pages 47-48)
- **Facebook for Business** — guided demonstrations of newly released features so users learn by tour, periodic newsletters with growth tips (pages 44-45)
- **Salesforce** — Trailhead gamified onboarding paths by role and product
