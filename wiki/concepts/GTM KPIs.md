# GTM KPIs

## Definition

GTM KPIs are the measurable indicators used to evaluate whether a Go-to-Market strategy is working. Pereira identifies ten core KPIs that span revenue impact, customer economics, market position, and operational efficiency — each tied to a different question the strategy should answer. The discipline is not just picking metrics but instrumenting them: collecting clean data from disparate sources, defining the same metric consistently across teams, and reviewing them on a cadence that allows course correction. The most common failure isn't picking the wrong KPIs — it's picking too many and reviewing none. A focused dashboard of 6-10 KPIs reviewed monthly beats 50 KPIs reviewed never.

## Key attributes

- **Ten core KPIs** — sales/revenue/growth, market share, CAC, LTV, ROI, retention/churn, conversion rates, brand awareness/perception, time to market, market penetration, partner/channel performance
- **Four KPI categories** — revenue impact, customer economics, market position, operational efficiency
- **LTV:CAC ratio is the unit economics test** — healthy ≥ 3:1
- **Leading vs lagging mix** — pipeline (leading) plus revenue (lagging) plus retention (deepest)
- **Cadence matters** — weekly for activity, monthly for outcomes, quarterly for strategy
- **Cross-functional definitions** — same metric must mean the same thing to sales, marketing, finance
- **Benchmarked against industry** — internal trends matter, but so do peer comparisons
- **Tied to SMART goals** — every KPI needs a target and a deadline, not just a number

## When to use

- Designing the GTM dashboard at launch time
- Reviewing GTM performance monthly / quarterly / annually
- Diagnosing why a GTM motion is underperforming (the KPIs point to the broken stage)
- Comparing campaigns, channels, or segments objectively
- Reporting to the board, investors, or executive team
- Sales / marketing / product alignment conversations

## When NOT to use

- As a substitute for qualitative customer understanding — numbers don't replace why
- For very early stage where sample sizes are too small for stable metrics
- When the metric becomes the goal (Goodhart's Law — metric gaming)
- For one-time use — KPIs only matter with sustained tracking and review cadence

## Common pitfalls

- Picking too many KPIs — 50 metrics no one reviews
- Conflicting definitions across teams — sales' "conversion rate" ≠ marketing's "conversion rate"
- Vanity metrics (page views, social followers) without conversion downstream
- Ignoring CAC payback period — strategies that look great on LTV but bleed cash
- Reviewing data without taking action — dashboards become wallpaper
- Failing to connect KPIs to specific GTM levers — knowing it's broken but not why
- Channel attribution wars — last-click vs first-click vs multi-touch
- Missing the brand metrics (awareness, perception) — only tracking conversion misses the leading wave

## Key metrics (the 10 core GTM KPIs)

- **Sales, Revenue, Growth** — the most fundamental: is GTM driving the desired outcomes?
- **Market Share** — competitiveness and penetration vs competitors
- **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)** — efficiency of acquisition spend
- **CLTV / LTV** — total revenue per customer over their lifetime; tracked against CAC
- **ROI** — net profit / total GTM investment
- **Customer Retention / Churn Rate** — retention is the leading indicator of long-term GTM health
- **Conversion Rates** — at every funnel stage (lead → MQL → SQL → opp → close)
- **Brand Awareness / Perception** — social mentions, sentiment, share-of-voice
- **Time to Market** — speed from strategy to availability; competitive timing
- **Market Penetration** — % of target market captured; new customers acquired
- **Partner / Channel Performance** — partner-sourced leads, revenue, MDF ROI

## Decision questions

- Which 6-10 KPIs would tell us in one glance if GTM is working?
- Do all teams (sales, marketing, finance) define each metric the same way?
- What's our LTV:CAC ratio, and what's the trend?
- Are we tracking leading indicators (pipeline, engagement) or only lagging (revenue)?
- What's our review cadence — and do we actually act on what we see?
- Which KPIs are vanity metrics, and which drive decisions?
- How do our metrics compare to industry benchmarks?

## Canonical examples

- **SaaS GTM dashboard** — MRR, ARR, NRR, CAC, LTV:CAC, payback period, churn, NPS
- **D2C dashboard** — CAC, AOV, LTV, repeat purchase rate, contribution margin
- **Enterprise sales dashboard** — pipeline coverage, win rate, ACV, deal velocity, quota attainment
- **Marketplace dashboard** — GMV, take rate, supplier/buyer LTV, liquidity ratios
- **Tesla early GTM tracking** — reservations, deliveries, brand sentiment, customer NPS (page 127-128)
