# Switching Costs

## Definition

Switching costs are the total cost — time, money, effort, risk — a customer incurs when moving from one provider's product to another's. High switching costs are a powerful retention mechanism: even when a competitor offers something better, the cost of switching may exceed the benefit. Switching costs can be deliberately engineered (proprietary data formats, ecosystem effects, contracts) or emerge naturally (learning curves, integration depth, accumulated history). Pereira frames it as one of Osterwalder's 7 key business model strength dimensions.

## Key attributes

- **Multi-dimensional cost** — time + money + effort + risk + emotional attachment
- **Engineered vs. emergent** — deliberate (data lock-in, proprietary formats) vs. natural (learning curves, accumulated history)
- **Asymmetric across customer segments** — small customers switch easily; enterprise customers face huge costs
- **Erodes over time** — competitors innovate to lower switching costs (data portability laws, migration tools)
- **Trade-off with customer anger** — high switching costs feel exploitative if not paired with ongoing value
- **Retention moat** — strong switching costs = low churn = high LTV

## When to use

- Defending a market position against new entrants
- Designing for retention in subscription / SaaS businesses
- Pricing power negotiations (high switching cost = pricing power)
- Customer relationship strategy
- Anti-defection initiatives in mature businesses

## When NOT to use

- As a substitute for ongoing customer value (lock-in alone is exploitative)
- In categories where competition law restricts lock-in mechanisms
- For commoditized products where customers expect easy switching

## Common pitfalls

- Building switching costs that feel exploitative (customer resentment + bad PR + regulatory risk)
- Underestimating customer willingness to suffer the switch when angry enough
- Confusing switching cost with product quality — if customers WANT to leave, they will eventually
- Letting competitor migration tools erode your switching cost moat
- Optimizing for switching cost so much that you lose product competitiveness

## Key metrics

- Churn rate (lower = high switching cost effect)
- LTV (longer relationship driven by switching costs)
- Switching cost estimate (qualitative or quantitative for enterprise)
- Customer satisfaction (high churn despite high switching cost = brewing crisis)

## Decision questions

- What are our switching costs, and are they engineered or emergent?
- Could we increase switching costs without crossing into exploitation?
- Where are competitors building bridges to lower the cost of leaving us?
- Is our retention driven by switching cost or genuine value? (The former is brittle.)

## Canonical examples

- **Apple ecosystem** — iCloud, iMessage, AirDrop, Apple Watch integration; deep lock-in (page 14)
- **iPod (historical)** — proprietary file format + iTunes lock-in
- **Nespresso** — proprietary coffee pod format (Bait and Hook + switching cost)
- **Game consoles** — game library doesn't transfer between PlayStation, Xbox, Nintendo (page 14)
- **Razor blade systems** — proprietary handle/blade fitment (page 14)
- **Printer cartridges** — same dynamic (page 14)
- **Enterprise SaaS** — integration depth, data accumulation, retraining cost
- **Bank accounts** — direct deposit, bill autopay, transaction history
