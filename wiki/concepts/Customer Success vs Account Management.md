# Customer Success vs Account Management

## Definition

Customer Success (CS) and Account Management (AM) both own ongoing customer relationships post-sale, but Pereira draws a sharp lens: AMs view client wellbeing through a **financial** lens (revenue, renewals, upsell, quota), while CS teams look at the **big picture** of outcomes (value realized, satisfaction, retention) (page 16). AMs are typically commissioned with sales targets and use sales/persuasion skills to drive expansion; CSMs are typically measured on outcome metrics (NRR, NPS, churn, health) and use empathy, listening, and collaboration to drive value. Some CS teams do carry MRR or expansion targets, "but this is rarely their main goal" (page 16). The 2022 US salary data Pereira cites — CSM ~$54,285 vs. AM ~$58,187 — reflects the difference: AMs earn more base and bonus from quota attainment; CSMs are measured on a broader, less commission-friendly set of outcomes.

## Key attributes (the contrast)

- **Primary lens** — CS: customer outcomes / value realized. AM: revenue, contract value, quota attainment
- **Posture** — CS: proactive (initiates contact, anticipates issues). AM: relationship-driven (renewals, upsell conversations)
- **Skill set** — CS: empathy, listening, critical thinking, communication, collaboration. AM: networking, marketing, leadership, interpersonal, sales
- **Compensation** — CS: salary-weighted, outcome bonuses. AM: salary + sales commission
- **Primary KPIs** — CS: NRR, NPS, churn, health, TTV. AM: bookings, renewal $, upsell revenue, quota %
- **Day-to-day** — CS: troubleshooting, implementation help, structural/process change support. AM: contract renewals, upgrades, loyalty cultivation for revenue
- **Origin** — CS: emerged from SaaS subscription economics. AM: long history in enterprise B2B sales
- **Failure mode** — CS that has quotas drifts toward AM; AM that has no outcome targets drifts toward order-taking

## When to use

- Designing a post-sale organizational structure — both roles or just one?
- Diagnosing why retention is weak despite "account management" coverage
- Hiring decisions — clarifying which role is actually needed
- Compensation design — aligning incentives with the desired outcomes
- Onboarding new leaders to a SaaS company who come from traditional sales backgrounds

## When NOT to use (the distinction)

- Very small organizations where one person plays both roles (force a choice later)
- Pure transactional businesses with no ongoing post-sale relationship
- When the real need is Customer Support — neither AM nor CSM fits
- Markets where the cultural expectation is "salesperson stays with the account" (no separation possible)

## Common pitfalls

- **Calling everyone a CSM** when half the team is hitting quota — sloppy taxonomy
- **Putting CSMs on sales quotas** to "make them earn their seat" — corrupts the role
- **No AM coverage at all** — CSMs ask for expansion poorly because they're not trained sellers
- **Customer confusion** — same person can't credibly be advisor and seller in the same conversation
- **Comp parity demanded** — CSMs often want AM-level total comp, but the role doesn't structurally support it
- **Handoff gaps** — AM closes deal, CSM owns delivery, neither owns the gray zone

## Key metrics (by role)

CS:
- NRR, GRR, churn rate, NPS, CSAT, health score, time-to-value, adoption

AM:
- New ARR, expansion ARR, renewal $, quota attainment %, pipeline coverage, deal velocity

Shared:
- Customer Lifetime Value, account-level retention, renewal close rate

## Decision questions

- What's the primary outcome we need from this role — outcomes or revenue?
- Are we structurally separating advisor (CS) from seller (AM), or collapsing into one role?
- Do our comp plans match the role's primary KPI?
- Where do CS and AM hand off — and who owns the gray zone?
- Are we over-indexing on AM (revenue) and under-investing in CS (retention)?

## Canonical examples

- **Salesforce** — explicit separation: Account Executives (AE) sell new business and expansion, Customer Success Group (CSG) owns adoption and retention
- **HubSpot** — segmented model: dedicated CSMs for retention, separate sales reps for expansion in larger accounts
- **Many early-stage SaaS** — single "Account Manager" role conflates both, then bifurcates as the company scales past $10-20M ARR
- **Gainsight** — built the playbook for the separation; runs both functions distinctly
