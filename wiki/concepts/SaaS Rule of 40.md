# SaaS Rule of 40

## Definition

The SaaS Rule of 40 is a business heuristic that asserts a healthy SaaS company's **growth rate plus profit margin should sum to at least 40%**. Pereira frames it as a tenet for testing whether a company is on pace to hit its revenue objective: "your company's growth rate and profit are inversely connected, and the sum of these two numbers will typically equal 40" (page 33). The math gives a single composite metric — a company growing 30% with a 10% profit margin scores 40; a company growing 60% with negative 20% margin also scores 40. Falling below 40 is a signal to revisit messaging or market sizing; clearing 40 with a profit margin above 40% is exceptional performance (page 33).

## Key attributes

- **Formula**: Growth Rate (%) + Profit Margin (%) ≥ 40 (page 33)
- **Composite metric** — collapses growth and profitability into one number
- **Tradeoff-aware** — explicitly acknowledges that growth and profit are often inversely related
- **Period-flexible** — typically YoY or trailing-twelve-months
- **Used pre-PMF as a viability check** — falling short signals message or market issues (page 33)
- **40 is not absolute** — "40 is not the only possible number to employ"; varies by product and market (page 33)
- **Below 40 is a diagnostic, not a death sentence** — prompts revisiting message or unmet-need definition
- **Investor heuristic** — widely used by SaaS-focused VCs and PE firms

## When to use

- Growth-stage SaaS performance review (Series B+)
- Investor pitches and board updates
- Comparing portfolio companies
- Tradeoff conversations between growth investment and profitability
- Quick sanity check on financial plans

## When NOT to use

- Very early stage (pre-revenue) — the math has no signal
- Non-SaaS businesses — the heuristic was built for recurring-revenue economics
- During one-time exceptional events that distort growth or margin
- As the only metric — pair with retention, LTV/CAC, and absolute scale
- For consumer / e-commerce — different unit economics

## Common pitfalls

- **Treating 40 as a hard cutoff** — the threshold varies by product and market
- **Using non-GAAP "profit" creatively** — adjusting until the score looks good
- **Chasing growth at any cost** — burning to hit 40 via growth alone destroys long-term value
- **Ignoring scale** — a $1M ARR company at 60% growth + 0% margin is very different from a $100M company at the same score
- **Confusing Rule of 40 with PMF** — meeting Rule of 40 doesn't mean you have fit; it means you have a viable engine

## Key metrics

- **Growth Rate** (%) — YoY or MoM revenue growth
- **Profit Margin** (%) — typically EBITDA margin or operating margin
- **Rule of 40 Score** — sum of the two
- **Pair with: NRR, LTV/CAC, CAC Payback** — for a fuller picture (see [[SaaS Metrics Suite]])

## Decision questions

- What is our current growth rate and profit margin?
- Where do we score on the Rule of 40?
- If we're below 40, is the issue messaging, target customer definition, or unmet-need scope? (page 33)
- Are we comfortable with the growth/profit tradeoff we're making?
- What number above 40 is appropriate for our specific market?

## Canonical examples

- **30% growth + 10% margin = 40** — Pereira's textbook example (page 33)
- **High-growth-stage SaaS** — often 60-80% growth with negative margin clearing 40
- **Mature SaaS** — often 15-25% growth with 20-30% margin clearing 40
- **Public SaaS comparables** — analysts publish Rule of 40 league tables regularly
