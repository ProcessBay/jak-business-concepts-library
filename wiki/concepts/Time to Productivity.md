# Time to Productivity

## Definition

Time to Productivity (TTP) is the elapsed time from a sales hire's start date until they consistently produce at expected performance level — typically defined as full quota attainment, but sometimes as "first closed deal" or "first month at-plan." Pereira treats it as the single most important onboarding KPI because every week shaved compounds across every future hire and translates directly to revenue capacity. Benchmarks vary by motion: SMB inside sales 1-3 months, mid-market B2B SaaS 3-6 months, enterprise complex sales 6-12 months. The metric requires precise definition (productive = ?), separation from "time to first deal" (a one-off can be lucky), and instrumentation that distinguishes the rep's contribution from territory, lead-flow, and product effects. Long ramps signal weak onboarding, weak coaching, weak content, weak product knowledge support, or hiring profile mismatch — but the diagnostic only works if TTP is measured cohort-by-cohort and not as a rolling average.

## Key attributes

- **Defined endpoint required** — "consistent quota attainment" vs. "first deal" vs. "month at-plan"
- **Cohort-based measurement** — same hire month tracked together, not rolling average
- **Confounders separated** — rep skill vs. territory quality vs. lead flow vs. comp
- **Benchmarked by motion** — SMB ≠ mid-market ≠ enterprise; B2B ≠ B2C
- **Leading indicators tracked** — certification, deal-flow milestones, manager scorecard
- **Tied to onboarding investment** — TTP improvement is enablement ROI
- **Visible to recruiting and finance** — hiring rate is constrained by ramp capacity
- **Decays with neglect** — every quarter without measurement, the number drifts up

## When to use

- Always — TTP is foundational; not tracking it means flying blind on hiring economics
- Quarterly review with sales leadership
- As input to revenue forecasting (new-hire contribution lags hiring date)
- As input to hiring plan (you can only hire as fast as you can ramp)
- When evaluating onboarding redesign — TTP delta is the success metric
- When making the case for enablement headcount or tooling

## When NOT to use

- As lone metric for evaluating individual reps (cohort context matters)
- Without segmenting by role, segment, region (averages hide reality)
- When sample size is too small (<5 reps in a cohort) for meaningful conclusions
- When territory or lead-flow conditions are atypical (need to normalize)

## Common pitfalls

- No agreed definition of "productive" — every department measures differently
- Reporting average TTP across all hires (hides cohort variation)
- Confusing "time to first deal" with TTP (one deal ≠ sustained productivity)
- Not separating rep contribution from territory/lead/comp effects
- Failing to track leading indicators (certification, manager scorecard) early
- Long-tenured reps and new hires lumped together in performance reports
- No mechanism to identify "stalled rampers" early — discovery happens at 6-month review
- Manager incentives don't reward ramp acceleration

## Key metrics

- **Days to first deal** — leading indicator for SDR/AE pipeline
- **Months to quota** — primary lagging metric
- **90-day quota attainment %** — onboarding milestone signal
- **Ramp velocity** — slope of productivity curve over months 1-12
- **Cohort comparison** — Q1 hires vs. Q2 hires vs. baseline
- **% of reps reaching productivity by benchmark** — % at-quota by month N

## Decision questions

- What's our definition of "productive" — first deal, first month at-plan, full quota?
- Is our TTP getting better, worse, or flat — and by cohort?
- How does our TTP compare to industry benchmarks for our motion and segment?
- Are we separating rep skill from territory, lead, and comp confounders?
- What's the financial cost of one week shaved off TTP, multiplied by hire rate?
- Who owns TTP improvement — enablement, sales leadership, both?
- Are we identifying stalled rampers in week 4, or week 24?

## Canonical examples

- **B2B SaaS healthy benchmark** — 3-6 months to quota for mid-market AE
- **Enterprise complex sales** — 6-12 months typical; 18+ for net-new logo motion
- **Salesforce** — invested in onboarding to shorten ramp; measurable revenue impact (pages 99-101)
- **HubSpot** — Sales Hub Academy + manager coaching system designed for shorter TTP (pages 97-99)
- **Pereira's framing** — TTP as critical onboarding KPI: tracking ramp-up speed, proficiency in product knowledge, contribution to revenue (pages 71-72)
