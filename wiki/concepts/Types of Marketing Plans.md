# Types of Marketing Plans

## Definition

Pereira documents seven distinct types of marketing plans, each serving a different function for different businesses at different times. The choice of plan type follows from the question being answered: are we organizing the year (annual/quarterly), launching a product (new product launch), generating demand pipeline (demand generation), focused on a single channel family (social media, content, SEO), or pursuing rapid, data-driven scaling (growth)? Each type shares the same core anatomy as a general marketing plan but emphasizes different elements — for example, the product launch plan has a three-phase structure (pre-launch / launch / post-launch), while the growth marketing plan emphasizes experimentation cadence and iteration.

## Key attributes

- **Quarterly or Annual** — comprehensive strategy outlining goals, tactics, and budget for 3-12 months; reviewed regularly
- **New Product Launch** — three-phase (pre-launch research → launch rollout → post-launch retention) plan tailored to product introduction
- **Demand Generation** — focuses on creating and nurturing interest across the full customer journey; combines content, email, social, SEO, paid
- **Social Media** — platform-specific plan covering content strategy, posting schedule, engagement activities, paid advertising
- **Content Marketing** — strategic document for creating and distributing valuable content to attract and retain a defined audience
- **SEO Marketing** — actions and techniques to improve a website's visibility and rank on search engines (Google, Bing)
- **Growth Marketing** — data-driven, results-focused approach combining marketing, product, customer acquisition, and analytics to identify and scale tactics that drive revenue
- **Same anatomy, different emphasis** — all share goals/audience/channels/budget/metrics but each weights certain elements differently

## When to use

- **Annual/Quarterly** — default for established businesses with predictable rhythm
- **New Product Launch** — when introducing a meaningfully new offering that needs market entry
- **Demand Generation** — when sales pipeline is the bottleneck and brand recognition exists
- **Social Media** — when target audience lives on specific platforms and brand awareness is the goal
- **Content** — when buyer journey involves research and education before purchase
- **SEO** — when organic traffic is a strategic asset (B2B SaaS, content-driven businesses)
- **Growth** — when business model is proven and the question is scaling, not validating

## When NOT to use

- **Single-channel plans** when the buyer journey demands omnichannel — picking only social or only SEO leaves the funnel half-covered
- **Growth marketing** before product-market fit — without PMF, the experimentation cadence amplifies wasted spend
- **Product launch plan** for incremental feature releases — overkill for non-meaningful changes
- **Annual plan** in a fast-pivoting startup where quarterly is more honest

## Common pitfalls

- **Choosing channel-specific plans without an umbrella marketing plan** — fragments the strategy
- **Mistaking growth marketing for general marketing** — growth requires experimentation discipline and analytics infrastructure
- **Building a content plan with no SEO plan** — content without distribution rarely earns ROI
- **Product launch plan that ignores post-launch retention** — Pereira: post-launch is the phase where customer feedback drives product iteration
- **Demand gen plan with no sales handoff** — leads generated but never qualified or worked

## Key metrics

- **Annual** — full marketing plan KPI suite (revenue, CAC, ROI, brand)
- **Product Launch** — pre-launch readiness, launch-week reach, post-launch retention, feature adoption
- **Demand Generation** — lead volume, MQL→SQL conversion, pipeline value
- **Social Media** — followers, engagement rate, share of voice, social-sourced leads
- **Content** — organic traffic, time-on-page, content-attributed conversions
- **SEO** — keyword rankings, organic sessions, domain authority, organic conversion
- **Growth** — experiment velocity, win rate, learning velocity, scaled-channel CAC

## Decision questions

- Which type best fits the current business question — launch, scale, or steady-state operation?
- Do we need an umbrella annual plan plus a sub-plan for a specific initiative?
- Is our growth-marketing readiness real (analytics, experimentation muscle), or aspirational?
- For a product launch, have we mapped all three phases (pre-launch, launch, post-launch)?
- For SEO/content, are we resourced for the 6-12 month payback window?

- **Annual marketing plan** — Pereira: "Outlines a company's marketing goals, tactics, and budget for a specific period of time, usually ranging from 3 to 12 months" (page 27)
- **Product Launch** — Pereira's three phases: pre-launch (research roadmapping), launch (rollout and reporting), post-launch (respondence and retention) (pages 28-30)
- **Demand Generation** — combines content, email, social, SEO, paid advertising; objective is brand awareness + leads + conversions (page 31)
- **Social Media** — Pereira: defines target audience, platform selection, content strategy, brand voice, posting schedule, engagement, paid options (page 31)
- **Content Marketing** — defines audience needs, content strategy, formats, distribution, measurement (page 32)
- **SEO Marketing** — keyword research, on-page, off-page, content, link building; goal = organic traffic + brand + conversion (page 33)
- **Growth Marketing** — data-driven; experiments across channels; continuous optimization (page 34)
