# Growing vs Scaling

## Definition

Growth and scaling are commonly confused but structurally different. **Growing** means investing in resources to increase productivity and revenue — costs and revenues rise together, roughly proportionally. **Scaling** means increasing productivity and revenue without proportional increase in investments and costs — more output with the same input. Pereira's contrast: e-commerce can sell 100 or 1,000 items with similar platform costs (scaling); a restaurant adding 10× more covers needs 10× more space, staff, and ingredients (growing). Tech/digital business models are more easily scalable because the digital is immaterial — investments don't enlarge as much as production.

## Key attributes

- **Growth = proportional** — output up means cost up at similar rate
- **Scaling = disproportional** — output up faster than cost up (rising margins)
- **Investment behavior differs** — growing requires capital for capacity; scaling requires capital for capability
- **Margin direction** — growth typically holds margins flat; scaling typically expands them
- **Industry-bound** — services-with-people grow; software/platforms scale
- **Hybrid common** — most firms have some scaling components and some growing components

## When to use

- Investor pitch — clarify whether your story is growth or scaling
- Strategic planning — different cost/capital strategies for growth vs scaling
- Diagnosing why a business with rising revenue has falling margins (it's growing, not scaling)
- Capital allocation decisions

## When NOT to use

- As a value judgment (scaling isn't always better — some great businesses grow linearly and serve their market well)
- When the firm's natural model is growth, trying to force scaling can break it
- For brand or community businesses where the relationship is the value (scaling breaks intimacy)

## Common pitfalls

- Marketing growth as scaling (most "scaling" companies are actually growing fast)
- Investing in scaling infrastructure for a growing business (overhead never amortizes)
- Investing in growing infrastructure for a scaling business (constrains the upside)
- Confusing top-line growth with margin scaling

## Key metrics

- Revenue growth rate vs. cost growth rate (delta = scaling signal)
- Headcount growth vs. revenue growth (low correlation = scaling)
- Marginal cost per incremental unit (falling = scaling)
- Gross margin trend across volume

## Decision questions

- Are we growing or scaling? Show me the cost-vs-revenue curve.
- Which parts of our business scale and which only grow?
- Where are we adding scaling-ready capability vs. growth-driven capacity?
- If we doubled revenue, would costs double — or less?

## Canonical examples

- **E-commerce platform** — sells 100 or 1,000 items at similar platform cost = scaling (page 10)
- **Restaurant chain** — 10 locations needs ~10× kitchens, staff, inventory = growth
- **Software SaaS** — additional users at near-zero marginal cost = scaling
- **Professional services firm** — additional clients require additional consultants = growth
