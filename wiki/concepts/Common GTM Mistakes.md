# Common GTM Mistakes

## Definition

Pereira catalogs the eight most frequent mistakes that undermine Go-to-Market strategies — failure modes seen across categories, company sizes, and decades. The pattern is consistent: most GTM failures aren't from bad ideas, they're from skipped fundamentals, misaligned execution, or refusal to adapt when reality diverges from plan. Pereira treats this as a checklist function: before launching or reviewing a GTM, walk the list and confirm you're not committing any of the eight. The discipline is preventive (catch these in planning) and diagnostic (when launch underperforms, the failure is usually one or more of these).

## Key attributes

- **Eight recurring failure modes** — lack of market research, undefined buyer personas, inadequate competitive analysis, overlooking distribution channels, misaligned messaging/positioning, premature/rushed launch, neglecting post-launch activities, ignoring data and analytics
- **Compounding effects** — mistakes interlock; one (e.g., bad personas) triggers others (misaligned messaging, wrong channels)
- **Detectable in advance** — every mistake has a leading indicator a disciplined team would catch
- **Pattern recognition function** — knowing the list helps spot the failure mode in someone else's struggling GTM
- **Often originates in skipped fundamentals** — the basics get rushed under pressure
- **Implementation challenges layer on top** — coordination, resourcing, market changes, talent gaps, stakeholder management, localization, tech integration, measurement
- **Risk mitigation is the companion discipline** — identify, quantify, contingency-plan

## When to use

- Pre-launch checklist — walk the eight before launch day
- Post-launch retrospective — diagnose which mistakes (if any) hurt the launch
- Coaching newer GTM teams — these are predictable, prevent rediscovery
- Investing / evaluating other companies' GTM — quick filter for execution risk
- Designing the GTM review cadence — each KPI maps to one or more mistakes

## When NOT to use

- As a substitute for first-principles thinking — checklists fail in novel categories
- For tiny iterations that don't constitute a real GTM
- As a way to blame the team after failure (use diagnostically, not punitively)

## The eight mistakes

1. **Lack of Market Research and Understanding** — diving in without comprehensive customer research; misguided assumptions about needs, preferences, pain points; building things nobody wants
2. **Undefined Buyer Personas** — vague targeting, inefficient resource allocation, generic messaging that resonates with no one
3. **Inadequate Competitive Analysis** — missing competitor strengths, weaknesses, pricing; direct clashes that could have been avoided; failure to differentiate
4. **Overlooking Distribution Channels** — one-size-fits-all channel choice; products not reaching intended customers through appropriate channels
5. **Misaligned Messaging and Positioning** — inconsistent or unclear message across channels; value proposition not effectively communicated; brand dilution
6. **Premature or Rushed Launch** — launching without proper testing or refinement; technical issues, incomplete features, lack of support resources; negative first impressions that compound
7. **Neglecting Post-Launch Activities** — treating launch as the finish line; failing customer support, updates, engagement; customer attrition that didn't have to happen
8. **Ignoring Data and Analytics** — no robust analytics, no KPI review; can't identify what's working or fix what isn't; strategy can't iterate

## Implementation challenges (layered on top of the eight)

- **Cross-departmental coordination failures** — sales, marketing, product, support running parallel rather than aligned
- **Resource allocation and budget mismanagement** — under-funding critical stages
- **Failure to adapt to market changes** — rigid plans in dynamic markets
- **Talent and skill gaps** — inadequate internal capability without partner or training investment
- **Stakeholder expectation management** — misaligned investor/board/customer expectations
- **Local and global variations** — applying one playbook across cultures and regulations
- **Technology integration complexity** — new tools or platforms causing disruption
- **Measurement difficulty** — unclear or absent KPIs prevent learning

## Decision questions (the checklist)

- Have we conducted real market research, not just internal speculation?
- Are our buyer personas specific enough to write copy against?
- Do we have a credible competitive landscape map?
- Have we evaluated multiple distribution channels and chosen deliberately?
- Is our message consistent across every channel and team?
- Are we launching because the product is ready, or because the calendar says so?
- Is our post-launch plan resourced as seriously as the launch itself?
- Do we have a KPI dashboard with a review cadence and decision rights?

## Canonical examples (negative)

- **New Coke (1985)** — research-driven product change misread emotional brand attachment (mistake #1 in reverse: research that asked the wrong question)
- **Google+** — pushed via Gmail / YouTube without earned demand; failed messaging and post-launch neglect
- **Microsoft Zune** — competitive analysis underestimated iPod's ecosystem lock-in
- **Quibi** — premature launch + misaligned positioning + neglected post-launch iteration
- **Many failed crypto / NFT launches** — pre-launch hype with no post-launch utility or engagement plan
