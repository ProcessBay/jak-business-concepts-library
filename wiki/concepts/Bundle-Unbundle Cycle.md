# Bundle-Unbundle Cycle

## Definition

The bundle-unbundle cycle is the observation — sharpened by Jim Barksdale's "there are only two ways to make money in business: bundling and unbundling" — that industries oscillate between bundled and unbundled configurations as technology and customer expectations shift. Pereira documents three full waves: (1) the **transportation wave** unbundled where things were made from where they were consumed, then enabled vertical bundling (Ford controlled iron mines to assembly plants); (2) the **IT/coordination wave** unbundled vertically integrated firms into specialists connected by virtual integration; (3) the **digital wave** neutralized gatekeepers, letting entrants offer individual components better than incumbents — and is now in its re-bundling phase as winners (Amazon Prime, Apple One, Disney+, Microsoft Game Pass) reassemble unbundled components into new bundles. The cycle implies that every successful unbundler eventually becomes a bundler, and every dominant bundle invites the next unbundler. The strategic question is not "which side wins" but "where in the cycle is this industry right now, and what does that imply for our positioning."

## Key attributes

- **Two-way pendulum** — industries swing between bundled and unbundled equilibria
- **Three documented waves** — transportation (1800s), IT/coordination (late 20th c.), digital (current)
- **Each wave is triggered by a cost collapse** — transport cost, communication cost, marginal-distribution cost
- **Unbundling phase** — niche specialists strip components from incumbents and serve them better
- **Re-bundling phase** — successful specialists reassemble adjacent components into new bundles (Spotify adds podcasts, Netflix adds gaming)
- **Asymmetric to incumbents at the turn** — the bundler-at-peak cannot easily unbundle without cannibalizing; the unbundler-at-peak struggles to bundle without losing focus
- **The cycle is industry-specific** — banking is mid-unbundling, streaming is mid-re-bundling, retail is in a complex multi-stage state
- **Both directions create wealth** — the Barksdale observation; bundling and unbundling are the same game viewed from opposite directions

## When to use

- As a positioning question for any new venture entering an established industry
- When evaluating an incumbent's defensibility — are they at the peak of their bundle's life?
- When evaluating a successful unbundler — are they about to face the next-generation unbundler, or have they earned the right to re-bundle?
- For long-range strategic planning where industry structure matters more than near-term tactics
- When sizing a market — the cycle phase determines who captures value
- As a lens for venture-capital pattern matching (Andreessen Horowitz's "platforms and verticals" thesis is a re-bundling argument)

## When NOT to use

- For short-term tactical decisions where the cycle's timescale (years to decades) doesn't apply
- In markets where regulation freezes the structure (utilities, healthcare in many jurisdictions)
- In markets too immature to have a clear bundle/unbundle dynamic yet
- As a deterministic prediction — the cycle's direction is observable in hindsight but ambiguous in the moment
- When the binary frame obscures more important structural questions (network effects, switching costs)

## Common pitfalls

- Treating the cycle as deterministic when it's a pattern, not a law
- Assuming the next phase is always the inverse — sometimes the same configuration persists for decades
- Missing the re-bundling moment because you assumed unbundling-forever (music streaming aggregators are now bundlers again)
- Building an unbundling thesis in an industry that's already mid-re-bundling
- Building a bundling thesis in an industry that's not yet been unbundled (over-engineered)
- Confusing this market-level cycle with intra-firm bundle/unbundle decisions
- Ignoring that the cycle's mechanism differs across waves (transport cost → coordination cost → distribution cost)

## Key metrics

- **Industry concentration (HHI)** — bundled markets concentrate; unbundling fragments them
- **Number of niche entrants per year** — the unbundling-phase signal
- **Average bundle share of revenue per incumbent** — rising = re-bundling phase
- **Time-since-last-major-unbundler** — long quiet periods often precede the next disruption
- **Cost-curve of the underlying enabler** — distribution cost, transport cost, coordination cost trend lines

## Decision questions

- Where in the cycle is our industry right now — bundled, unbundling, or re-bundling?
- What cost is collapsing right now that might trigger the next phase change?
- If we're an incumbent, are we at the peak of our bundle's life — and if so, what's our internal-unbundling plan?
- If we're an unbundler, have we earned the right to re-bundle adjacent components yet?
- Is our positioning consistent with the cycle phase, or are we fighting the tide?
- What does the second-order play look like — when the cycle turns again, where do we want to be?

## Canonical examples

- **First wave (transportation, 1800s)** — railroads/canals unbundled production from consumption location, then enabled vertical bundling (Ford 1900s-1950s) (pages 64-66)
- **Second wave (IT/coordination, late 20th c.)** — falling communication costs broke up vertical integration; Ford-style conglomerates unbundled into virtual integration (page 65-66)
- **Third wave (digital, current)** — gatekeepers neutralized; iTunes unbundled albums, Craigslist unbundled into Airbnb/Etsy/Tinder/Indeed, Stripe unbundled banks
- **Re-bundling phase examples** — Amazon Prime (bundles video, music, photos, gaming, shopping), Apple One (Music + TV+ + Arcade + iCloud), Disney+ (re-bundled Disney IP into one subscription), Microsoft Game Pass (bundled console + 100+ games)
- **Music industry full cycle** — bundled albums → unbundled singles (iTunes) → re-bundled into all-you-can-stream subscriptions (Spotify)
- **Television full cycle** — bundled cable channel packages → unbundled streaming services → re-bundling (Disney+/Hulu/ESPN+ bundles, Max+Discovery)
- **Banking mid-unbundling** — Stripe (payments), Plaid (data), Chime (deposits), Wealthfront (investments) — re-bundling not yet clear winner
- **Retail mid-re-bundling** — Amazon's everything-store thesis re-bundles unbundled e-commerce verticals; Shopify enables continued unbundling
