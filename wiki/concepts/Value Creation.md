# Value Creation

## Definition

Value creation is the process of turning labor and resources into something that meets the needs of others — tangible goods, services, experiences, or ideas. Pereira frames it as the foundational purpose of a business and argues it must be measured from three perspectives simultaneously: customers (does the offer meet their needs and exceed expectations?), employees (does the work environment foster engagement, growth, and well-being?), and investors (does the model produce sustainable returns?). A firm that creates value for only one of the three is unstable.

## Key attributes

- **Three-stakeholder model** — value must be created for customers, employees, AND investors simultaneously; single-stakeholder optimization is fragile
- **Process orientation** — value creation is an ongoing process, not a one-time event
- **Differentiation vehicle** — it is the mechanism by which a firm differentiates itself in a crowded market
- **Both tangible and intangible** — applies to physical goods, services, brand experiences, knowledge, ideas
- **Mutual relationship** — the value is co-created with customers, not delivered to them as passive recipients
- **Society/environment context** — modern value creation increasingly includes positive externalities, not just internal returns

## When to use

- Designing a new offering or business model
- Evaluating whether an existing line is sustainable
- Aligning leadership across customer, employee, and investor lenses
- Diagnosing why a profitable firm is bleeding talent or losing brand loyalty (look for missing stakeholder)
- Auditing a strategy presentation — "for whom is value being created here?"

## When NOT to use

- As a vague slogan in a deck without specifying for whom and how — produces empty corporate-speak
- As a substitute for measurable customer outcomes
- When the immediate need is operational fix, not strategic positioning

## Common pitfalls

- Treating shareholder return as the only metric (Pereira's three-perspective framing is the corrective)
- Confusing value creation (positive-sum) with value capture (zero-sum extraction from customers)
- Optimizing for short-term customer satisfaction while burning out employees or starving R&D
- Claiming "we create value" without articulating the specific mechanism

## Key metrics

- Customer: NPS, retention rate, share of wallet, repeat purchase rate
- Employee: engagement score, voluntary turnover, internal promotion rate
- Investor: ROIC, free cash flow, total shareholder return
- Brand: unaided awareness, brand equity index

## Decision questions

- For whom are we creating value — and is that the same group that pays us?
- Which of the three stakeholders are we underinvesting in?
- What would a customer say is the value our product creates in their life?
- Could we describe our value creation in one specific sentence that a competitor couldn't say?
- What externalities are we creating, positive or negative?

- **Apple** — innovation-driven value creation (iPhone, iPad disrupting their categories) (page 14)
