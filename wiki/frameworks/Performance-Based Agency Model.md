# Performance-Based Agency Model

## Definition

A performance-based agency ties some or all of its compensation to client outcomes — leads generated, revenue produced, ROAS hit, signups acquired — rather than to hours worked or scope delivered. Pereira lists "performance-based fees" as one of the four primary revenue streams alongside retainers, project fees, and partnerships (pages 82–83) and frames performance pricing as one of the structures to consider when designing agency pricing: "performance-based pricing, where incentives are linked to delivering specific results" (page 87). The model trades certainty of revenue for upside aligned with client value, and is most defensible when the agency controls a single channel whose outputs are clearly attributable.

## Key attributes

- **Outcome-linked fees** — CPL, CPA, %-of-revenue, ROAS bonus, or hybrid floor + bonus
- **Tight attribution** required — agency must own the channel to claim the credit
- **Risk-sharing** — agency carries some downside, captures upside
- **High accountability narrative** — easier sales pitch ("we win when you win")
- **Cash-flow volatility** — revenue swings with client results and seasonality
- **Typically capped or floored** — pure pay-on-performance is rare; hybrids are common
- **Best for single-channel specialists** — PPC, paid social, affiliate, SEO with conversion tracking

## When to use

- Channels with clean attribution: paid media, affiliate, conversion-rate optimization
- Clients with mature analytics and trusted measurement
- E-commerce, lead-gen, and direct-response verticals
- When the agency is confident in its ability to outperform a baseline
- When you want a differentiating sales narrative against retainer-only competitors

## When NOT to use

- Brand work, awareness campaigns, content marketing where attribution is murky
- Clients with poor tracking — disputes about what "counts" will kill the relationship
- Multi-touch B2B sales cycles longer than your patience for waiting on payment
- Agencies without the balance sheet to absorb a bad quarter
- When client product/offer/operations bottleneck results outside the agency's control

## Common pitfalls

- Taking performance deals on channels you don't actually control
- Attribution wars — the agency claims it, client says it would have happened anyway
- Pricing the performance bonus too low, the floor too high (you absorb downside, miss upside)
- Misaligned KPIs — bonus on leads, but client only values closed revenue
- Client gaming the inputs (cutting product price, weakening offer) and blaming the agency
- Concentration in performance deals making agency revenue dangerously volatile

## Key metrics

- **ROAS** (Return on Ad Spend) by client and campaign
- **Cost per Acquisition / Cost per Lead** vs. agreed thresholds
- **% of revenue from performance bonus** vs. fixed floor
- **Performance Bonus Hit Rate** — % of months you earn the upside
- **Client Lifetime Value** (page 95)
- **Gross Margin** on performance contracts vs. retainer contracts
- **Revenue Volatility** — std dev of monthly revenue

## Decision questions

- Do we actually control the channel enough to claim performance credit?
- Is the attribution model agreed in writing, with shared dashboards?
- What's the floor we need to cover cost-of-delivery if the bonus never triggers?
- Are we taking performance deals because we believe in the work, or because we couldn't sell a retainer?
- Can our balance sheet absorb 2–3 bad quarters across the performance book?

## Canonical examples

- **Affiliate-style PPC shops** — pure performance, %-of-spend or %-of-revenue
- **Single Grain / Tier 11** — performance-leaning paid media agencies
- **CRO specialists** — fee-plus-uplift-bonus contracts for ecommerce
- **KSA e-commerce performance shops** — small Riyadh/Jeddah specialists charging Saudi D2C brands (Nice One, Floward, Nahdi e-commerce) on ROAS-tiered deals
- **Snapchat / TikTok performance boutiques in MENA** — short-form video shops running CPI / CPA campaigns for gaming and FMCG advertisers
- **Memac Ogilvy performance practice** — performance-bonus layered on retainer for large KSA advertisers
