# Issue-Based Strategic Planning

## Definition

Issue-Based Strategic Planning is a short-cycle (6-12 month) planning model that identifies the specific issues currently constraining an organization and designs programs to resolve them, rather than building a long-range strategic frame from vision down. Pereira frames it as a "contemporary and subsequent plans" approach — guided by tools and analysis to understand what the actual issues are, then organizing teams and stakeholders around concrete action and adjustment criteria. It's the right model when the organization knows it has problems but doesn't yet have the stability or clarity to commit to a 3-5 year strategic plan.

## Key attributes

- **Short-cycle horizon** — typically 6-12 months, not the 3-5 years of conventional strategy
- **Issue-first, not vision-first** — starts from "what's broken or stuck" rather than "where do we want to go"
- **Group and structure analysis** — uses workshops, root-cause analysis, and structured diagnosis
- **Action and adjustment focus** — emphasizes plans the team can actually execute, not aspirational direction
- **Budget and decision criteria built in** — explicit thresholds for when to revise
- **Pragmatic and adaptive** — designed for organizations not yet ready for long-range commitments
- **Bridge to fuller strategy** — often a precursor to Basic Model or Cascade when issues stabilize

## When to use

- When the organization is in turnaround, distress, or rapid change
- For early-stage startups where committing to 3-5 year strategy is premature
- When prior strategic plans have failed and trust in long-range planning is low
- During leadership transitions when the new leader needs quick wins before a full strategy
- When external conditions are too volatile for long-cycle planning
- As a first cycle before graduating to a fuller model

## When NOT to use

- When the organization is stable and ready for long-range strategic commitments
- For mature firms where a strategic frame already exists and just needs execution
- When issues are symptoms, not causes — short-cycle thinking can entrench root-cause neglect
- As a permanent state — organizations need a long-range view eventually
- When leadership uses "we're issue-based" as cover for avoiding hard strategic choices

## Common pitfalls

- Treating symptoms rather than root causes — issue lists multiply without resolution
- Confusing issue-based with crisis management (issue-based is structured; crisis is reactive)
- Letting the issue list grow indefinitely — needs explicit prioritization
- Failing to graduate to a longer-range frame once issues stabilize
- Stakeholder lists who own issues but lack authority to resolve them
- No budget or decision criteria — issues identified but resourcing left vague
- Using issue-based to dodge vision-setting permanently

## Outputs / signals of good use

- A prioritized list of the 5-10 most consequential issues facing the organization
- Each issue has a named owner, a resolution plan, and a budget
- Decision criteria for when to revise the plan are explicit
- Stakeholder alignment on which issues matter most
- Issues actually resolved over the 6-12 month cycle
- Successor plan — either a new issue-based cycle or graduation to fuller strategy

## Decision questions

- What are the 5-10 issues most constraining our organization right now?
- For each issue — what's the root cause, who owns it, what's the resolution plan?
- Have we resourced the resolutions with budget and decision authority?
- What are the criteria for revising or escalating?
- Are we addressing causes or just symptoms?
- When do we exit issue-based mode and adopt a fuller strategic frame?

## Canonical examples

- **Turnaround companies** — Issue-Based is standard during distress periods
- **Newly funded startups** — pre-PMF organizations rarely benefit from long-range strategy
- **Pereira's framing** — "identifying business problems and coming up with critical solutions to solve them" (page 63)
- **Post-crisis reorganizations** — bridges from crisis to durable strategy
- **PE-acquired companies in year 1** — quick-win identification before a multi-year value creation plan
