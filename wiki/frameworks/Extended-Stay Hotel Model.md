# Extended-Stay Hotel Model

## Definition

Extended-stay hotels and serviced apartments provide hotel-style hospitality for stays measured in weeks or months rather than nights — fully furnished suites or apartments with kitchenettes/full kitchens, separate living areas, laundry, and housekeeping. Pereira treats them as a distinct hospitality model because guest economics flip: average length of stay becomes the central metric, ADR per night drops (often with weekly/monthly rates) but utilization rises and acquisition cost per booked night collapses. The model targets relocating professionals, project-based consultants, expatriate families, business travelers on long assignments, and increasingly digital nomads and bleisure travelers — segments traditional hotels serve clumsily.

## Key attributes

- **Suite or apartment format** — kitchen, separate living area, more storage, workspace
- **Length-of-stay pricing** — discounted weekly/monthly rates trade ADR for utilization and lower acquisition cost
- **Lower staffing intensity per room** — less daily housekeeping, no banquet/F&B complexity
- **Self-service amenities** — in-unit laundry or on-site facilities, kitchen, gym
- **Corporate contracts** — material share of revenue from B2B agreements (relocation, consulting, project crews)
- **Community-style touches** — social events, networking, sense of "home" for long-stayers
- **Hybrid revenue mix** — room nights + ancillary services (housekeeping packages, airport transfers, concierge)
- **Bleisure-friendly** — accommodates blended business + leisure stays

## When to use

- Markets with concentrated corporate relocation, consulting, or project-team activity
- Cities hosting long-cycle construction, infrastructure, or oil/gas projects (highly relevant in KSA's giga-project era)
- Locations near hospitals (medical-tourism long stays, family visits)
- Markets with growing remote-work / digital-nomad demand
- Operators with thin operating teams who can't sustain F&B-heavy full-service properties

## When NOT to use

- Pure leisure destinations with predominantly short-stay tourist demand
- Locations without the corporate, project, or relocation demand base to fill long-stay inventory
- Markets where short-term rental platforms (Airbnb) have already captured the long-stay segment at a price you can't match

## Common pitfalls

- Marketing it as a hotel — short-stay distribution channels don't reach long-stay buyers
- Underinvesting in unit quality (kitchens, work setup, storage) — long-stayers notice everything
- No B2B sales motion — without corporate contracts, occupancy stays too lumpy
- Treating cleaning frequency the same as a regular hotel (cost without value)
- Ignoring community programming — long-stayers leave when they feel isolated, raising churn
- Pricing weekly/monthly rates without modeling true utilization economics

## Key metrics

- **Average Length of Stay (ALOS)** — the model's defining metric
- **Occupancy Rate** — must run consistently high to compensate for blended-rate discount
- **ADR** (blended across nightly / weekly / monthly bands)
- **RevPAR / GOPPAR**
- **Corporate contract share of revenue** — proxy for booking-channel stability
- **Repeat-stay rate** — long-stayers often return for the next project

## Decision questions

- Is there enough corporate, project, or medical-driven long-stay demand to sustain ALOS targets?
- Do our units actually work for living, not just sleeping?
- Have we built the B2B sales motion to secure anchor corporate contracts?
- How do we compete with Airbnb in the long-stay segment — service, security, expensable receipts?
- What's the right balance of short-stay (higher ADR) and long-stay (higher utilization) revenue?

## Canonical examples

- **Marriott Residence Inn, Hilton Homewood Suites, Hyatt House** — major chain extended-stay brands
- **Staybridge Suites, Candlewood Suites** (IHG) — extended-stay specialists
- **Adagio (Accor), Citadines, Frasers Hospitality** — global serviced-apartment operators
- **Rotana Hotel Apartments, Arjaan by Rotana** — leading MENA aparthotel operator with strong KSA footprint
- **Ascott (Singapore)** — global serviced-apartment platform expanding into Saudi
- **Selina** — extended-stay-meets-coworking targeting digital nomads (page 94)
