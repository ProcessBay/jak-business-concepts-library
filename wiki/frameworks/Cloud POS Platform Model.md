# Cloud POS Platform Model

## Definition

Cloud POS platforms replace traditional on-premises point-of-sale software and hardware with a subscription-based, cloud-hosted SaaS system accessed via a web browser or tablet app, paired with integrated payments. Pereira distinguishes four POS business models (pages 39-42): **Traditional on-premises** (capex software + hardware + support fees); **Cloud-based / SaaS POS** (monthly subscription, scalable, auto-updated, integrated with payments); **Mobile POS / mPOS** (smartphone/tablet as the terminal, often free download + per-transaction fee); and **Integrated e-commerce + POS** (single platform unifying online and offline channels, the omnichannel-enabler). The cloud variant has eaten the category since 2010 because it removes the capex hurdle, slashes deployment time, and bundles payments — which is where the platform actually makes money.

## Key attributes

- **Subscription-based access** — monthly/annual fee per location, register, or user; no capex
- **Cloud-hosted, auto-updated** — no on-premises servers; new features push continuously
- **Payments-integrated** — Stripe-style or proprietary payment processing is the lucrative attach
- **Hardware-light** — tablet + card reader + receipt printer replaces $5k legacy register
- **Real-time multi-location visibility** — single inventory, sales, and customer view across stores
- **App-extensible** — third-party apps for loyalty, accounting, inventory, scheduling
- **Multi-channel by design** — POS + e-commerce + mobile order on one ledger
- **Vertical specialization wins** — restaurant POS, retail POS, salon POS, cannabis POS have different needs

## When to use

- SMB and mid-market merchants displacing legacy terminals (Verifone, NCR, Aloha)
- Geographies where card payment penetration is rising and merchants need EMV/contactless
- Verticals where modern features (online ordering, kitchen display, mobile pay) are competitive table stakes
- When you can attach payments — without payments, the unit economics are typically thin
- Multi-location chains needing centralized reporting and inventory
- Pop-up retail, food trucks, mobile services (mPOS variant)

## When NOT to use

- Enterprise retailers with $10M+ custom POS investments and complex integrations
- Geographies with poor connectivity (cloud POS needs reliable internet; offline mode is a band-aid)
- Categories with regulatory specialization that horizontal POS can't cover (pharmacy, fuel, gaming)
- When you cannot get payments licensed or partnered in the target market
- When the merchant's legacy POS is bundled with critical loyalty/CRM the new vendor can't replicate

## Common pitfalls

- Selling subscription cheap to win the deal, then losing money because payments don't attach
- Underestimating the support burden of cloud POS during peak hours — outages are existential
- Vertical mismatch — generic POS pushed into restaurants without KDS, table management, or split-check
- Hardware certification — every region has different EMV/payment terminal certifications
- Ignoring connectivity reality — offline mode that loses transactions is worse than no offline mode
- Competing on price with horizontal incumbents when vertical specialization is the real moat

## Key metrics

- **MRR / ARR** (subscription)
- **Payment attach rate** (% of merchants on platform payments) — the leading indicator of unit economics
- **GMV processed** through platform payments
- **Take rate** on payments (typically 2.6-2.9% + per-transaction fee)
- **Hardware attach rate** and hardware margin
- **Logo and revenue churn** (SMB POS churn is structurally 1-3% monthly)
- **ARPU expansion** from add-ons (loyalty, gift cards, capital, payroll)

## Decision questions

- Horizontal or vertical-specialized — and which vertical(s) earn us defensibility?
- Do we own payments or partner — and how does that change unit economics?
- What's our hardware strategy — own-brand, certified third-party, or BYO tablet?
- How do we win against incumbent terminal providers who own merchant relationships through banks/processors?
- What add-ons (loyalty, capital, payroll, e-commerce) expand ARPU beyond the base subscription?

## Canonical examples

- **Square (Block)** — pioneered mPOS for SMB; payments-led monetization
- **Shopify POS** — extends Shopify's e-commerce platform into physical retail; the integrated-e-commerce+POS variant (pages 57-59)
- **Toast** — restaurant-vertical cloud POS; specialized for F&B (KDS, online ordering, tips, payroll)
- **Lightspeed** — multi-vertical cloud POS for retail, restaurant, golf
- **Clover** (Fiserv) — cloud POS distributed through bank/ISO channel
- **Foodics** — Saudi/MENA restaurant POS leader
- **Rewaa** — Saudi retail cloud POS
- **Bayan POS** — Saudi POS provider for SMB retail
- **Loyverse** — free mPOS targeting micro-merchants in emerging markets
