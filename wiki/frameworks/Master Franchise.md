# Master Franchise

## Definition

A master franchise grants a master franchisee the exclusive right to operate and develop a franchise system within a defined territory (typically a country or large region), including the right to sub-franchise individual units to local franchisees. Pereira describes the master franchisee as effectively a sub-franchisor: they sell franchises, train and support local franchisees, enforce brand standards, run marketing, and collect a share of franchise fees and royalties — in exchange for a substantial master franchise fee and ongoing royalty payments to the originating franchisor. The model is the dominant vehicle for international expansion, because the master franchisee brings local market knowledge, regulatory navigation, language fluency, and cultural adaptation that a distant franchisor cannot match. Distinct from area development, where the franchisee operates units directly without sub-franchising.

## Key attributes

- **Exclusive territory rights** — typically country or large multi-state region
- **Right to sub-franchise** — master franchisee sells units to local franchisees
- **Master franchisee as sub-franchisor** — runs training, support, marketing, audits locally
- **Layered economics** — master fee upfront + royalty share between franchisor and master
- **Local market expertise critical** — language, culture, regulation, real estate knowledge
- **Heavy operational responsibility** — master must build local franchisor infrastructure
- **Common for international expansion** — dominant vehicle for cross-border franchising
- **Complex legal structure** — international laws, IP protection, disclosure across jurisdictions

## When to use

- International expansion where local knowledge is mission-critical
- Franchisor lacks the resources or appetite to build country-level operations
- Target market has regulatory, cultural, or language barriers difficult to navigate remotely
- A well-capitalized, operationally sophisticated local partner is available
- Long-term territory commitment justifies the master franchise fee

## When NOT to use

- Markets where direct franchisor operation is feasible
- Small territories that don't justify the master franchise overhead
- Categories where centralized control of standards is critical and can't be delegated
- When no qualified master franchisee candidate exists (settle and you'll regret)
- Industries with rapid change where master franchisee can't keep pace

## Common pitfalls

- Choosing the wrong master franchisee — hard to unwind a long-term exclusive contract
- Master franchisee under-invests in support; sub-franchisee quality erodes
- Cultural adaptation drifts too far from brand standards
- Royalty split disputes between franchisor and master
- IP protection failures in the master's jurisdiction
- Master franchisee can't recruit qualified sub-franchisees fast enough
- Termination is legally and operationally complex when master underperforms

## Key metrics

- Master franchise fee paid
- Sub-franchise fee and royalty share between franchisor and master
- Number of sub-franchised units opened (vs. development schedule)
- Territory AUV
- Sub-franchisee satisfaction
- Brand consistency audit scores across the territory
- Master franchisee P&L health

## Decision questions

- Is this market truly inaccessible to direct franchisor operation?
- Do we have a master franchisee candidate with capital, ops capability, and cultural fit?
- What master fee + royalty split aligns incentives long-term?
- How do we enforce brand standards across a third-party-run territory?
- What's our termination path if the master underperforms?

## Canonical examples

- **McDonald's India** — joint venture master franchise structure with Bakshi Group and Connaught Plaza Restaurants; 400+ restaurants (page 56)
- **Starbucks China** — President Chain Store Corporation as JV master franchisee; 4,000+ stores (page 56)
- **Domino's Pizza Enterprises** — Bain Capital JV master franchise across Australia, NZ, Japan, Europe; 2,000+ stores (page 56)
- **Marriott / Hilton international master franchise structures** — country/region masters
- **Saudi/MENA: Americana** — historical master franchise relationships across MENA for QSR brands; **Alshaya** — master franchisee for many Western retail/F&B brands across the Gulf; **Apparel Group** — Gulf master franchisee for apparel brands
