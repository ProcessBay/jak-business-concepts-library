# Social Commerce Platform

## Definition

Social commerce platforms collapse the gap between discovery and checkout by embedding shopping inside social feeds — the user sees a product in an Instagram post, taps once, and buys without leaving the app. Pereira identifies it as a distinct e-commerce platform type (page 35-36) alongside hosted, open-source, and marketplace models. Monetization typically combines a platform take rate on transactions with advertising revenue and partnership fees from merchants and brands. The platform leverages the social network's existing engagement, user-generated content, and influencer ecosystem to drive purchase intent at the moment of discovery — fundamentally different from search-driven e-commerce where intent precedes the visit.

## Key attributes

- **Discovery-driven, not intent-driven** — purchases triggered by feed exposure, not search query
- **Native checkout** — buying happens inside the social app, not via redirect to a merchant site
- **Influencer/UGC engine** — creators, reviewers, and friends are the merchandising layer
- **Embedded inside social network** — leverages existing time-on-app and social graph
- **Hybrid revenue** — transaction fees + advertising + sponsored content + creator commission share
- **Lower friction, higher impulse** — single-tap purchase reduces cart abandonment dramatically
- **Trust through social proof** — comments, likes, shares act as live reviews
- **Platform owns the customer** — merchant rarely gets buyer's email or repeat-purchase data

## When to use

- Visually-driven categories where the image sells the product (fashion, beauty, home, food)
- D2C brands with strong creator or influencer relationships and content engines
- Markets with high social media penetration and low e-commerce maturity (SE Asia, MENA, LATAM)
- Impulse-purchase price points (typically <$100) where buyers don't research extensively
- When your acquisition cost on Google/Meta ads has crossed the breakeven line — social commerce can lower CAC

## When NOT to use

- Considered purchases requiring spec comparison, reviews, or long research cycles
- B2B or enterprise sales
- Categories with high return rates that erode platform economics
- When you depend on owning the customer relationship (social platforms gate that data)
- When your brand cannot operate at social-media velocity (rapid drops, content cadence, replies)

## Common pitfalls

- Treating social commerce as another ad channel rather than a content-merchandising operation
- Underinvesting in creator/influencer relationships — the actual GTM engine
- No native checkout integration — sending users to an external site collapses the conversion
- Platform dependency risk — algorithm changes or policy shifts can erase a business overnight
- Inability to retain customers — without email/SMS, repeat purchase relies on the platform's feed
- Over-discounting to compete on social — destroys margins and brand positioning

## Key metrics

- **In-feed conversion rate** (impressions → purchase)
- **GMV** transacted through social commerce surface
- **Social CAC** vs paid search CAC
- **Influencer attribution** — revenue per creator, per post, per code
- **Take rate** charged by the social platform
- **Average order value** and impulse-purchase share
- **Repeat purchase rate** (low in social commerce; main weakness)

## Decision questions

- Is our category visual and impulse-driven enough to thrive in-feed?
- Can we build a content/creator engine, or are we trying to bolt social onto a static catalog?
- How do we capture customer identity for repeat purchase outside the platform?
- What's the platform risk concentration — too much GMV on one social network?
- Does the platform's take rate plus ad costs still leave us a viable margin?

## Canonical examples

- **TikTok Shop** — the breakout social commerce model 2023-2025; in-feed video + checkout + live shopping
- **Instagram Shopping / Facebook Shops** — Meta's social commerce surfaces (page 35-36)
- **Pinterest Buyable Pins** — discovery-driven, intent-leaning social commerce (page 35-36)
- **WeChat / Mini Programs** — Chinese super-app social commerce baseline
- **Snapchat Spotlight + AR try-on** — Snap's commerce surface
- **YouTube Shopping** — creator-led commerce attached to video
- **Snapchat / TikTok in MENA** — high engagement among Gulf youth driving Saudi D2C brands
