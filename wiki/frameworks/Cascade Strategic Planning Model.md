# Cascade Strategic Planning Model

## Definition

The Cascade Strategic Planning Model is a structured top-down approach that decomposes an organization's strategy into six progressively more concrete elements: **Vision Statement → Values → Focus Areas → Objectives → KPIs → Projects**. Each layer is tightly coupled to the next — projects ladder up through KPIs to objectives, focus areas, values, and vision — making it easy to trace any tactical activity back to strategic intent. Pereira presents Cascade as one of the best strategic-planning models available today, distinguished by its KPI rigor (each objective requires at least two KPIs) and its lightweight governance: monthly strategic reports, project updates, and KPI exception management.

## Key attributes

- **Six-layer cascade** — Vision → Values → Focus Areas → Objectives → KPIs → Projects
- **Vision-to-project traceability** — every project ladders back to vision
- **KPI rigor** — minimum two KPIs per objective, measurable and time-bounded
- **Focus Areas as the bridge** — narrow the vision to the 3-5 things that actually matter this cycle
- **Lightweight governance** — monthly strategic reports + ad-hoc project updates + KPI exception triggers
- **Owner-centric** — the organization's owner narrates overall progress; each layer has clear accountability
- **KPI Exceptions as a control mechanism** — when KPIs fall outside tolerance, an exception report explains and prescribes corrective action

## When to use

- For SMBs and scale-ups that need structure without the heavy machinery of Hoshin or Balanced Scorecard
- When strategy is clear but execution keeps drifting (Cascade's traceability surfaces the drift)
- For founder-led companies where the vision needs to be explicitly cascaded
- When the leadership team wants a single artifact connecting vision to weekly work
- During a strategic reset — Cascade is fast to set up and easy to communicate
- When the firm has KPI discipline but lacks a strategic frame around it

## When NOT to use

- For very large enterprises that need the layered alignment of Hoshin Kanri
- When the strategy itself is uncertain (Cascade assumes a clear vision and stable focus areas)
- In hyper-dynamic environments where KPIs and projects shift faster than the cascade can update
- As a substitute for OKRs at team level (Cascade is the strategic frame; OKRs can sit inside Objectives)
- When the leadership team won't sustain the monthly review discipline

## Common pitfalls

- Vision too generic to cascade meaningfully (everything qualifies as "in line with the vision")
- Too many Focus Areas — defeats the purpose of narrowing
- KPIs that measure activity, not outcome (vanity metrics; see [[Vanity Metric Trap]])
- Projects multiplying without retiring older ones — the cascade becomes a junkyard
- Monthly reports become narrative-only with no decisions attached
- KPI exceptions ignored or normalized — the control mechanism fails silently
- Confusing Cascade with a project tracker — it's a strategy execution model, not a PMO tool

## Outputs / signals of good use

- A single one-page artifact showing Vision → Values → Focus Areas → Objectives → KPIs → Projects
- Every employee can trace their current project up through KPIs and objectives to a focus area
- Monthly strategic report cadence with explicit decisions, not just status
- KPI exception reports that result in actual corrective action
- Periodic pruning — projects and KPIs retired as objectives evolve

## Decision questions

- Does every active project trace cleanly up to vision via the cascade?
- Do all our objectives have at least two well-chosen KPIs?
- Which Focus Areas no longer serve the vision, and should be retired?
- Are our monthly strategic reports producing decisions, or just status?
- When KPIs fall outside tolerance, are we triggering exception reports — and acting on them?
- Where in the cascade is the weakest link between layers?

## Canonical examples

- **Founder-led scale-ups** — Cascade fits the natural top-down rhythm without the bureaucracy of BSC
- **PE-backed portfolio companies** — Cascade's KPI rigor + monthly reports align with sponsor expectations
- **Pereira's structural element list** — Vision → Values → Focus Areas → Objectives → KPIs → Projects, with governance via monthly reports, project updates, and KPI exceptions (pages 71-73)
