# Inbound Sales Methodology

## Definition

Inbound sales is a buyer-centric sales methodology that aligns the seller's process to the customer's buying journey rather than to internal sales-team conventions. Pereira frames it as "reverse prospecting" — the buyer initiates contact (via website, content, free trial, or referral), and the salesperson's job is to identify, connect, explore, and advise rather than to pitch and push. Inbound sales pairs tightly with inbound marketing: marketing produces content that attracts qualified prospects, sales engages them with context already in hand. The methodology emerged as buyer access to online information collapsed the seller's traditional information advantage. Inbound salespeople spend less time on cold outreach and more time on personalized, situationally-aware engagement built on the prospect's actual digital footprint.

## Key attributes

- **Buyer's journey first** — sales process designed after mapping Awareness → Consideration → Decision
- **Four-stage seller process** — Identify (find leads) → Connect (engage) → Explore (diagnose) → Advise (recommend)
- **Personalized first contact** — every outreach references buyer-specific context (industry, role, recent activity)
- **Sequences over scripts** — multi-channel cadences (email/phone/social) tailored per persona
- **Insight-led, not feature-led** — leads with diagnosis, not product pitch
- **Lead prioritization hierarchy** — inbound leads → inbound companies → trigger events → social → connections → passive
- **Trust through helpfulness** — value delivered before any ask
- **Lower cost than outbound** — leverages organic and content channels

## When to use

- Prospects organically arrive via website, email signups, content, free trials
- Long, considered B2B sales cycles where buyer education matters
- SaaS and services with rich content marketing already in place
- Markets where buyers self-educate before contacting sales
- Teams with strong marketing/sales alignment ("Smarketing")
- When CAC efficiency matters more than short-term volume

## When NOT to use

- Brand-new SaaS company with no awareness or audience (start outbound)
- Markets where buyers do not search online for solutions
- Highly transactional, low-consideration purchases
- When you need rapid revenue (inbound is slower to compound)
- Categories with no content moat to build

## Common pitfalls

- Designing the sales process around internal stages instead of the buyer's journey
- Defaulting to demo-mode the moment a prospect expresses interest (no exploration)
- Generic outreach disguised as "personalization" (just merging a first name)
- Reps chasing passive buyers instead of leads showing real interest signals
- Misaligned sales and marketing (no SLA on lead volume or definition)
- Treating inbound and outbound as either/or rather than complementary
- No process for trigger events (social mentions, web visits, hashtag activity)

## Key metrics

- **Lead quality / MQL-to-SQL conversion** — % of marketing leads sales accepts
- **Sales cycle length** — days from first touch to close
- **Win rate** — closed-won / total qualified opps
- **CAC** — Customer Acquisition Cost (lower than outbound when at scale)
- **Reply / engagement rate** on personalized outreach
- **Demo-to-close rate** — proxy for fit at presentation stage

## Decision questions

- Are we attracting enough qualified inbound to feed a sales team, or are we starving them?
- Do reps have the digital-footprint data (web visits, content reads) to personalize first contact?
- Is our marketing producing content for all three buyer-journey stages, or only one?
- Have we mapped the buyer's journey before designing the sales process?
- Can a rep explain why this prospect, today, in their own situation?
- Are sales and marketing operating under a shared SLA?
