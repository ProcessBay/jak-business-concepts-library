# Business Model Space Assessment

## Definition

A four-domain framework for analyzing the external environment a business model operates within: **Industry Forces** (competitors, new entrants, substitutes, stakeholders, suppliers/value chain), **Market Forces** (market issues, segments, needs & demands, switching costs, revenue attractiveness), **Key Trends** (technology, regulatory, socioeconomic, societal/cultural), and **Macro-Economic Forces** (global market conditions, capital markets, commodities/resources, economic infrastructure). Pereira adapts it from Osterwalder & Pigneur's strategy work as a companion to Business Model Canvas — BMC describes what your model IS; this framework describes the SPACE it operates in.

## Key attributes

- **Four domains** — Industry, Market, Trends, Macro-Economic
- **Forces, not facts** — each domain captures dynamic forces, not static descriptions
- **Companion to BMC** — provides external context that BMC alone doesn't show
- **Question-driven** — each sub-domain has 5-8 specific questions to answer
- **Compatible with Porter** — Industry Forces overlap with Porter's 5 Forces
- **PESTEL adjacent** — Key Trends + Macro overlap with PESTEL

## When to use

- Pre-launch market entry analysis
- Annual strategic review beyond the model itself
- M&A and partnership decisions requiring environmental scan
- Identifying disruption risk (new entrants, substitutes, regulatory)
- Scenario planning over 3-5 year horizons

## When NOT to use

- For pure operational decisions disconnected from environment
- When the team will produce a heavy doc without action follow-through
- In stable, mature markets where the environment is well-known

## Common pitfalls

- Treating it as a one-time exercise rather than ongoing scan
- Listing forces without identifying actionable threats and opportunities
- Conflating Industry Forces with Macro-Economic Forces
- Missing emerging substitutes (often the biggest threat is from outside your current industry)
- Failing to translate forces into business-model implications

## Key metrics

- # of identified threats and opportunities
- Action-conversion rate (forces → strategic moves)
- Scenario coverage (% of plausible futures considered)
- Cross-functional alignment on key forces

## Decision questions per domain

**Industry Forces:**
- Who are competitors, new entrants, substitutes?
- Who in our value chain has leverage over us?

**Market Forces:**
- What customer needs are emerging or shifting?
- Where is demand growing or declining?
- What are switching costs and revenue attractiveness?

**Key Trends:**
- What tech, regulatory, socioeconomic, cultural trends affect us?
- Which represent opportunity vs. threat?

**Macro-Economic Forces:**
- Global market conditions (boom/bust)?
- Capital availability and cost?
- Commodity prices and trends?
- Quality of economic infrastructure?

## Canonical examples

- **Telecom incumbents vs. Skype** — failed to identify Skype as a substitute force (page 18)
- **Music labels vs. streaming** — underestimated Spotify/Apple Music as new entrants
- **Retail vs. Amazon** — substitute force from e-commerce
- **Taxis vs. Uber** — new entrant, regulatory disruption
