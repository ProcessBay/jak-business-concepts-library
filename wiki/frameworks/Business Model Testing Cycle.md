# Business Model Testing Cycle

## Definition

The Business Model Testing Cycle is a 7-step iterative process for validating business model assumptions before market launch: (1) Develop a Business Model Concept (via BMC); (2) Identify Underlying Assumptions; (3) Build Falsifiable Hypotheses; (4) Choose a Test Format; (5) Develop the Test Setup; (6) Execute the Test; (7) Analyze Results and Update. Pereira frames this as essential to avoid the common entrepreneur trap of falling in love with an idea and skipping validation — the lack of testing turns out far more costly in lost time and capital than rigorous testing would have been. The cycle is closely related to Build-Measure-Learn (lean startup) but framed around business-model-level assumptions rather than product features alone.

## Key attributes

- **Seven sequential steps** — concept → assumptions → hypotheses → format → setup → execute → analyze
- **Iterative, not linear** — Step 7 feeds back to Step 1 indefinitely
- **Hypothesis-driven** — assumptions become testable hypotheses with falsification criteria
- **Multi-method** — different tests fit different assumption types (interviews, prototypes, MVPs, A/B tests)
- **Three risk areas tested** — Desirability, Feasibility, Viability
- **Speed compounds** — short cycles let teams test many hypotheses; faster learning beats more capital

## When to use

- Pre-launch validation of new ventures
- Major pivots in existing businesses
- New product/feature additions to mature products
- After significant market shifts requiring assumption re-checking
- Post-mortem: examining why a failed launch failed

## When NOT to use

- For pure execution where the model is validated
- When team falls in love with the testing process instead of shipping
- When customer feedback won't actually change decisions (testing for show)

## Common pitfalls

- Confusing assumptions with facts (Step 2 failure)
- Building hypotheses that aren't falsifiable (Step 3 failure)
- Choosing test formats that won't actually test the hypothesis (Step 4 failure)
- Skipping Step 7 — running tests without learning from results
- Testing low-impact assumptions while ignoring critical ones
- Treating testing as a single-pass exercise

## Key metrics

- # of hypotheses tested per cycle
- Validation rate (% confirmed vs. falsified)
- Time per cycle (target: weeks, not months)
- Pivot count after testing
- Cost of testing vs. cost of building unvalidated

## Decision questions

- For our top 5 assumptions: how would we know we're wrong?
- Are we testing the highest-impact assumptions first?
- What's our cycle time, and could we make it faster?
- When did we last update our business model based on test results?
