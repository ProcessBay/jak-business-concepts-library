# Digital Maturity Model

## Definition

The Digital Maturity Model (DMM), associated by Pereira with Deloitte's formulation, assesses an organization's digital maturity across **five dimensions: Customer, Strategy, Technology, Operations, and Organization & Culture**. It is a diagnostic instrument used at the start of a digital transformation (DX) journey to evaluate current state, identify gaps, and prioritize investment. Maturity is not a single number — it is a five-axis profile that exposes asymmetric strengths (e.g., strong Technology but weak Culture) which would otherwise be hidden by an aggregated score. Pereira positions DMM as one of several diagnostic frameworks alongside HCL's four-stage DTF (Discover→Envision→Enable→Scale), Capgemini's Four Digital Forces (Experience, Automation, Connectivity, Analytics), and the broader Digital Readiness Assessment Framework (which adds Performance Measurement, Governance, Innovation/Agility, and Data Management as separate axes).

## Key attributes

- **Five dimensions** — Customer, Strategy, Technology, Operations, Organization & Culture
- **Multi-axis profile**, not single score — exposes asymmetric maturity
- **Diagnostic, not prescriptive** — names gaps; choice of remedy is downstream
- **Maturity stages typically 1–5** (Ad-hoc → Optimizing); Pereira references the structure without locking a stage taxonomy
- **Companion to readiness assessment** — overlaps with IT Infrastructure, Data Management & Analytics, Digital Channels, Competitive Landscape evaluation
- **Repeated re-measurement** — DX is ongoing, so maturity is re-scored periodically

## When to use

- Pre-transformation: to baseline current state before vision-setting and KPI definition
- M&A or restructuring: to assess what digital assets/gaps the combined org will carry
- Board/investor reporting: to communicate transformation progress on a stable rubric
- Multi-business-unit organizations: to compare BU readiness and target investment
- KSA Vision 2030 contexts: to align org maturity against National Transformation Program and DGA-style maturity benchmarks

## When NOT to use

- When the organization has no executive commitment to act on findings (assessment becomes shelfware)
- As a standalone deliverable — DMM scores without roadmap and governance produce paralysis
- For very small organizations where lightweight gap-analysis is sufficient
- When the assessment is captured by the IT function alone — distorts toward Technology dimension

## Common pitfalls

- **Single-score reduction** — collapsing the five-axis profile into one number erases the asymmetry that makes DMM useful
- **Technology-dimension bias** — IT-led assessments overweight infrastructure and underweight Culture and Customer
- **No re-assessment cadence** — done once and forgotten
- **Benchmarking obsession** — comparing scores across industries with different baselines misleads
- **Skipping straight to remedy** — picking tools before understanding which dimension is the binding constraint

## Key metrics

- **Maturity score per dimension** (1–5 or 1–10 scale, organization-defined)
- **Dimension balance** — variance across the five axes; high variance signals strategic risk
- **Year-over-year delta** per dimension
- **Capability gap index** — distance from target maturity, weighted by strategic priority
- **Readiness Index** for specific DX programs (composite of relevant dimensions)

## Decision questions

- Which dimension is our binding constraint — is it Culture, not Technology?
- Are we mature in Technology but immature in Customer understanding (a common 2022-era profile)?
- What is the target maturity per dimension at end of the strategy horizon?
- How often will we re-score, and who owns each dimension?
- Does our readiness profile match the DX strategy we are about to fund?

## Canonical examples

- **Deloitte client engagements** — DMM is the assessment instrument behind many of Deloitte's published DX case studies (page 42)
- **HCL Digital Transformation Framework (DTF)** — alternative four-stage model (Discover, Envision, Enable, Scale) (page 41)
- **Capgemini's Four Digital Forces** (Experience, Automation, Connectivity, Analytics) — alternative dimensional model (page 41)
- **NEOM / Tonomus (KSA)** — cognitive-city platform built greenfield, used DMM-style assessments to define baseline capabilities for partner ecosystem
- **SDAIA (Saudi Data & AI Authority)** — operates a national-level maturity framework for KSA government entities; conceptually analogous to enterprise DMM
- **Ministry of Health (KSA)** digitization program — used multi-dimensional readiness assessment before rolling out Seha and Sehhaty platforms
- **DGA (Digital Government Authority, KSA)** — Government Maturity Index follows the same multi-axis logic (Strategy, Capabilities, Services, User Experience)
