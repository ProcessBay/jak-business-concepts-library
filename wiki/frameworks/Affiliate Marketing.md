# Affiliate Marketing

## Definition

Affiliate marketing is a business model where third-party publishers promote another company's products and earn a commission on resulting sales or actions. Three parties make the model work: the seller (merchant), the affiliate (publisher referring traffic), and the affiliate network (which manages tracking, payments, and attribution). Pereira places it as a foundational online business model — particularly valuable for content sites, bloggers, and influencers, and for merchants who pay only on results.

## Key attributes

- **Three-party structure** — seller, affiliate (publisher), affiliate network
- **Performance-based** — affiliates earn only when their referral converts (sale, signup, lead)
- **Tracking via cookies/links** — referral links carry tags identifying the affiliate
- **Cookie window** — typical 30-90 day attribution period after click
- **Tiered commission** — common 5-50% of sale value, depending on category
- **Scales without inventory** — affiliates don't handle product; just referrals

## When to use

- Building a content business with monetizable audience (blogs, YouTube, newsletters)
- E-commerce expansion without retail spend (Amazon-style affiliate program)
- Software with sales-led GTM augmented by affiliate referrals
- Categories where customers research before purchase (high-consideration goods)
- Where the firm wants pay-for-performance vs. brand-spend

## When NOT to use

- Low-margin products that can't sustain commission payouts
- Categories where consultative selling is critical (high-touch B2B)
- When the brand can't afford the loss of customer-relationship ownership
- When affiliate fraud is hard to detect (gaming, low-quality leads)

## Common pitfalls

- Choosing wrong commission structure — too low (no affiliate interest) or too high (eats margin)
- Cookie-stuffing or last-click attribution abuse by aggressive affiliates
- Brand exposure via low-quality affiliate placements (spam, misleading content)
- Slow payment cycles that lose top affiliates
- Confusing affiliate program with general advertising

## Key metrics

- Affiliate-driven revenue share of total
- Conversion rate from affiliate traffic
- EPC (earnings per click) for affiliates
- Cookie window length
- Click-through rate on affiliate links
- Active affiliate count

## Decision questions

- What commission rate balances affiliate interest with our margin requirements?
- What cookie window best captures our actual buying cycle?
- Are top affiliates aligned with our brand or detracting from it?
- Should we run our own affiliate program or join an affiliate network?

## Canonical examples

- **Amazon Associates** — the prototype; 1-10% commission across product categories (page 21)
- **Rakuten Marketing** — major affiliate network (page 21)
