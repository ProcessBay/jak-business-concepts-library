# Wholesaling (Real Estate)

## Definition

Real-estate wholesaling is a capital-light, intermediary strategy: the wholesaler signs a purchase agreement with a motivated seller (typically a distressed property), then — before the contract closes — assigns the contract to an end-buyer (usually another investor) for a higher price. The wholesaler never takes title, never makes repairs, never carries the mortgage; their margin is the assignment fee (the spread between the agreed purchase price and the assignment price). Pereira positions wholesaling as the lowest-barrier entry into real-estate investing — no license required, no rehab capital, no operating overhead — and contrasts it against flipping, which requires ownership, capital, and renovation expertise. The model lives or dies on two assets: (1) a sourcing engine for motivated sellers / distressed properties, and (2) a buyer list of cash-ready investors who can close fast.

## Key attributes

- **No title transfer** — wholesaler assigns the contract, doesn't own the asset
- **Capital-light** — earnest money deposit only (often <$1,000); no rehab funds, no carrying costs
- **No license required** — in most US jurisdictions (varies by state; some require disclosure)
- **Speed-dependent** — must find an end-buyer before the original contract closes
- **Margin = assignment spread** — typically $5k–$30k per deal
- **Two-sided sourcing problem** — motivated sellers on one side, investor buyers on the other
- **Backout clause critical** — contract must let the wholesaler walk if no buyer surfaces
- **Reputation-dependent** — repeat business with the same investor buyers depends on deal quality

## When to use

- Aspiring real-estate investor with no capital but with hustle, marketing skill, and patience
- Investor wants to learn the market and build a network before deploying capital
- Direct-mail / driving-for-dollars / cold-outreach are part of the toolkit
- Markets with high volume of distressed / off-market / inherited / pre-foreclosure inventory
- Strong local network of active flippers, BRRRR investors, and landlords ready to buy

## When NOT to use

- Markets with thin distressed inventory (most listings are MLS-priced)
- Wholesaler has no buyer list and no plan to build one
- Local regulation requires a real-estate license to assign contracts (check state law)
- Wholesaler lacks the marketing budget / time to source consistently
- Risk-averse personality — wholesaling is high-volume, high-rejection

## Common pitfalls

- **No buyer list** — the most common failure; sellers walk when assignment dates slip
- **Overpaying the seller** — destroys the spread before the deal starts
- **Underpricing the assignment** — leaves money on the table
- **Missing the backout clause** — wholesaler personally liable if no buyer found
- **Ethical / legal gray zones** — misrepresenting intent to seller can trigger regulatory action
- **Burning seller relationships** — bad faith negotiations kill referral pipeline
- **Treating it as easy money** — it's a marketing-intensive business with low close rates

## Key metrics

- **Assignment fee per deal** — average $5k–$30k
- **Deals per month** — full-time wholesalers target 2–4
- **Buyer list size** — qualified, cash-ready investors
- **Marketing cost per lead** — direct mail, PPC, bandit signs, SEO
- **Lead-to-contract rate** — typically 1–3% of motivated-seller leads convert
- **Contract-to-close rate** — % of signed contracts that successfully assign

## Decision questions

- Do I have (or can I build) a list of 30+ cash-ready investor buyers in my market?
- What's my sourcing channel — direct mail, PPC, networking, driving for dollars?
- Is my market deep enough in distressed inventory to support 2–4 deals/month?
- Is my state's regulatory framework wholesaler-friendly or does it require licensing?
- What's my contingency if the contract closes and I can't find a buyer?

## Canonical examples

- **Pereira's $80k → $90k example** — wholesaler buys distressed home at $80k, assigns at $90k, nets $10k without taking title (page 46)
- **BiggerPockets / REIClub communities** — central wholesaling education and network ecosystems
- **HomeVestors / "We Buy Ugly Houses"** — franchised wholesaling / quick-buy network in the US
- **Saudi/MENA**: formal wholesaling barely exists in KSA — the dominant analogous activity is **broker-led off-market matchmaking** between owners and developers/family offices. As the Real Estate General Authority (REGA) tightens broker licensing and the secondary-market platform **Ejar** institutionalizes, true assignment-style wholesaling may emerge. **Wafi** (off-plan sales regulator) currently constrains anything that looks like contract-flipping on new builds
