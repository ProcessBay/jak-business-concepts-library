# Grants and Subsidies for Startups

## Definition

Grants and subsidies are non-repayable financial assistance provided by governments, foundations, industry associations, and non-profits to support startups — typically in innovation, R&D, social impact, or strategic-industry categories. Pereira positions them as uniquely attractive because they impose neither debt nor equity dilution: founders keep ownership and avoid repayment obligations. The trade-offs are application burden (long, detailed, low success rates), reporting and accountability requirements post-award, and eligibility narrowness — grants typically restrict use of funds to specified activities. Winning credible grants also delivers validation and credibility signals that attract follow-on investment.

## Key attributes

- **Non-repayable** — no debt, no equity, no dilution
- **Restricted-use** — funds tied to specified R&D, market entry, or innovation activity
- **Long application cycles** — weeks to months from submission to decision
- **Validation signal** — winning a competitive grant signals legitimacy to VCs
- **Reporting obligations** — post-award milestones, financial reports, audits
- **Eligibility-gated** — sector, geography, founder profile, project type constraints
- **Stackable** — grants typically compatible with other funding sources

## When to use

- Deep-tech, R&D-intensive, or scientific-research startups
- Industries with active grant ecosystems (clean energy, healthcare, AI, manufacturing)
- Pre-revenue stage when dilutive capital is expensive
- Social-impact or mission-driven ventures with foundation alignment
- Market-entry or expansion into geographies with subsidy programs
- Founders willing to invest in application quality and reporting infrastructure

## When NOT to use

- Working-capital needs where speed matters — grant timelines are too slow
- Founders without the bandwidth to manage application and reporting cycles
- When restricted-use constraints don't match the actual funding need
- Ineligible category (most grants don't fund pure software SaaS)
- When the grant amount doesn't justify the application and compliance cost

## Common pitfalls

- Underestimating application effort — strong applications take weeks
- Winning a grant whose restricted use forces strategic detours
- Reporting overhead crowding out founder time on the business itself
- Treating grants as primary funding strategy in categories where they don't reliably land
- Failing to align project scope with grant criteria, leading to compliance failures
- Hidden conditions (IP rights, government claw-backs, milestone clauses)

## Key metrics

- **Grant size** — varies wildly: $10K microgrants to $10M+ R&D programs
- **Application success rate** — typically 5-30% for competitive programs
- **Time to disbursement** — 3-12 months from submission to first tranche
- **Effective hourly rate** — grant amount / founder hours invested
- **Reporting burden** — hours/month on compliance post-award
- **IP and equity claw-back terms** — what the funder retains

## Decision questions

- Does our project genuinely match the grant program's stated objectives?
- Can we afford the application time investment given our runway?
- Are the restricted-use terms compatible with our actual strategic needs?
- What ongoing reporting and compliance burden does this create?
- Does winning this grant strengthen our position with future investors?

## Canonical examples

- **US SBIR/STTR** — federal R&D grants for tech startups, Pereira's reference category
- **Government grants** — local, regional, national programs across industries (page 31)
- **Research grants** — biotech, energy, IT focus from foundations and agencies (page 31)
- **Incubator/accelerator subsidized programs** — workspace, mentorship, microgrants (page 31)
- **Industry-specific grants** — agriculture, healthcare, renewable energy (page 32)
- **Saudi context — NTDP (National Technology Development Program)** — flagship Saudi grant for tech startups; covers R&D, commercialization, and growth milestones; ProcessBay/Utopian relevant
- **Saudi context — Monsha'at SME grants** — General Authority for Small and Medium Enterprises programs
- **Saudi context — MCIT (Ministry of Communications) digital programs** — sector grants for ICT, AI, IoT initiatives
- **Saudi context — KAUST Innovation Fund** — academic-industry grants for deep tech
