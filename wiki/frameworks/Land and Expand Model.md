# Land and Expand Model

## Definition

Land and Expand is a B2B SaaS strategy where the company first "lands" individual users or small teams inside an organization via a free product or low-friction adoption, then "expands" by approaching the organization's leadership to upgrade to enterprise-wide paid plans once enough seats are in active use. Pereira identifies this as a distinct freemium variant — the payment decision moves from individual users to organizations, and ads/marketing target the specific market segment where enough adoption has organic momentum. Examples: Atlassian, Twilio, Veeva, Asana, Slack, Yammer, AWS — all proved bottom-up adoption converts into enterprise contracts.

## Key attributes

- **Two-phase motion** — Land (individual/team adoption) → Expand (org-wide contract)
- **Bottom-up adoption** — users pull the product into the organization, not procurement pushing it down
- **Internal network effects** — once enough seats adopt, the org-level deal becomes obvious
- **Sales-marketing handoff** — initial free-tier adoption is product-led; expansion is sales-led
- **Different payer** — free user ≠ paying customer (org executives pay)
- **Long contracts at expansion** — enterprise SaaS contracts (typically 1-3 year, $50K-$5M ARR)
- **Net Revenue Retention compounds** — expansions within accounts add to MRR

## When to use

- B2B SaaS where individual user value is genuine
- Categories where users can adopt without IT/procurement approval (web-based, freemium tier)
- Collaboration / productivity tools where adoption is contagious within teams
- When organic word-of-mouth within companies is realistic
- When the company can fund the long expansion cycle (months to years to convert)

## When NOT to use

- Procurement-gated categories (security, compliance, regulated industries)
- Heavy-touch enterprise software requiring deep customization
- B2B where individual employee can't adopt without org approval
- When sales cycle requires immediate contract (Land-Expand is patient capital)

## Common pitfalls

- Treating Land and Expand as freemium (it's a B2B-specific freemium variant with different mechanics)
- Failing to track adoption-to-enterprise-conversion handoff
- Letting individual users multiply without enterprise outreach
- Pricing the enterprise tier too high — orgs hit budget objections after free adoption
- Failing to instrument "expansion-ready" signals (seat count, usage depth, team adoption)
- Over-investing in inside sales before bottom-up adoption is real

## Key metrics

- Seats per account (rising = ready to expand)
- Account expansion rate (% of accounts converting from free to paid)
- Time-to-team-adoption (how fast a team reaches expansion threshold)
- Net Revenue Retention (NRR) — Land-Expand companies aim for 120%+
- Enterprise contract conversion rate (from free accounts)
- ACV (Annual Contract Value) post-expansion

## Decision questions

- For each free account: how many seats are active, and is it ready to "expand"?
- What's our expansion threshold trigger (X seats, Y% engagement)?
- Do we have a sales motion to monetize bottom-up adoption?
- What expansion-blocking issues are emerging (security, admin features, billing)?

## Canonical examples

- **Slack** — free workspaces gain team adoption; enterprise tier unlocks history + admin + SSO (page 15)
- **Atlassian** (Jira, Confluence, Trello) — free tier, premium tier, enterprise tier; well-documented Land-Expand (page 15)
- **Twilio** — developers integrate API on dev accounts; enterprise expands to production scale (page 15)
- **Asana** — team adoption → org-wide expansion (page 15)
- **AWS** — devs use AWS free tier; orgs expand to enterprise agreements (page 15)
- **Yammer / Veeva** (page 15)
