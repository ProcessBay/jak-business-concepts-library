# HubSpot Inbound Flywheel

## Definition

The HubSpot Inbound Flywheel is a three-stage marketing-and-revenue framework — Attract, Engage, Delight — that HubSpot adopted (replacing its earlier funnel) to operationalize the Flywheel Effect for inbound marketing organizations. Where a funnel treats customers as output and then forgets them, the HubSpot flywheel makes delighted customers the *fuel* for the next turn: they generate referrals, reviews, and case studies that lower CAC and feed back into Attract. It is the dominant flywheel template for content-led B2B SaaS go-to-market.

## Key attributes

- **Three stages** — Attract (content draws strangers), Engage (tools convert leads), Delight (success creates promoters)
- **Customer at the center** — every team rotates around CX, not down a funnel
- **Friction is the enemy** — every handoff between stages should reduce, not add, friction
- **Force and friction model** — apply force (good marketing, great product) and remove friction (bad handoffs, slow support) to spin faster
- **Cross-functional ownership** — marketing, sales, and customer success share the wheel
- **Compounds CAC payback** — delighted customers reduce paid acquisition dependency over time
- **Content as the perpetual flywheel input** — high-quality educational content is the primary Attract engine

## When to use

- B2B SaaS and content-led businesses building a long-term demand engine
- Organizations replacing siloed marketing/sales/CS funnels with a unified motion
- Teams with the patience to compound — inbound flywheels take 6–18 months to spin
- Categories where buyers self-educate (technical, considered purchases)
- Products with strong NPS where promoter advocacy is realistic

## When NOT to use

- Categories where buyers can't or won't self-educate (impulse, low-consideration)
- Businesses needing immediate pipeline (outbound or paid is faster to start)
- Products with structurally low NPS (delight stage will be empty)
- Markets where content is already saturated and differentiation is impossible
- Organizations that can't sustain content investment for the 12+ months before compounding

## Common pitfalls

- Writing thin content and calling it Attract — without quality, the wheel never starts
- Skipping Delight (no investment in CS) and wondering why referrals don't compound
- Measuring each stage with siloed KPIs that incentivize friction at handoffs
- Treating Engage as a sales-only stage and excluding marketing/CS
- Expecting funnel-speed results from a flywheel motion
- Failing to remove friction between stages — broken handoffs stall the wheel

## Key metrics

- **Organic traffic** and **content engagement** — Attract health
- **Lead-to-customer conversion rate** and **sales cycle length** — Engage efficiency
- **NPS**, **CSAT**, **customer health score** — Delight quality
- **Referral rate / customer-sourced pipeline** — proof the wheel is feeding itself
- **CAC payback period** — should compress as the flywheel matures
- **Net Revenue Retention (NRR)** — expansion from delighted customers

## Decision questions

- What is our single best Attract asset, and how often is it refreshed?
- Where is the worst friction in our Attract → Engage → Delight handoffs?
- Are marketing, sales, and CS measured against the same flywheel outcome?
- What % of new pipeline comes from existing-customer advocacy?
- Have we underinvested in Delight relative to Attract and Engage?
- Are we ready for a 12+ month investment horizon before compounding shows?

## Canonical examples

- **HubSpot** — the framework's namesake; built a $20B+ company on Attract/Engage/Delight (pages 26-27)
- **Drift** — conversational marketing built around inbound flywheel mechanics
- **Notion** — content + community + product-led growth feeding the same wheel
- **Atlassian** — low-touch inbound model that compounds via developer advocacy
- **Buffer** — transparent content marketing as the perpetual Attract engine
