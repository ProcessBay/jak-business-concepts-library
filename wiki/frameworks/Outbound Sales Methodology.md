# Outbound Sales Methodology

## Definition

Outbound sales is the seller-initiated motion in which the salesperson actively reaches out to prospects who have not yet expressed interest, attempting to compress the path from cold to qualified opportunity. Pereira frames it as the fastest route to revenue for early-stage SaaS and B2B firms whose product is unknown — outbound generates leads in the time it takes to start dialing, where inbound demands months of content gestation. The methodology spans cold calling, cold email, social selling, live chat outreach, podcast guesting, webinars, IP-tracker-driven follow-up, influencer collaboration, and segmented email automation. Outbound is more expensive than inbound (paid delivery, headcount-heavy) but unmatched for speed and for reaching ICPs that don't self-identify online. Best run as a six-step system: segment → hypothesize → gather data → classify into ICPs → score segment attractiveness → mine sub-segments for leads.

## Key attributes

- **Seller initiates first contact** — opposite of inbound's pull motion
- **Multi-channel tactical mix** — cold call, cold email, social DM, live chat, referrals, podcasts, webinars, influencers
- **Six-step system (Raffi)** — segmentation → hypotheses → data gathering → ICP classification → segment scoring → sub-segment mining
- **ICP-driven** — works only when the Ideal Customer Profile is sharply defined
- **Personalization at scale** — segmentation + templating; pure mass-blast performs poorly
- **Faster time-to-revenue than inbound** — leads possible from day one
- **Higher CAC** — paid channels and headcount drive cost
- **CRM-dependent** — outbound at scale needs systematic tracking and sequencing

## When to use

- Early-stage SaaS with no inbound flywheel yet
- Expanding into a new segment where existing inbound doesn't reach
- ABM (Account-Based Marketing) against a named-account list
- Markets where buyers don't search (highly technical, niche, regulated)
- When you need revenue this quarter, not next year
- Complement to inbound — most mature orgs run both

## When NOT to use

- Commodity B2C purchases (annoying to consumers, low conversion)
- When ICP is unclear — outbound to "everyone" guarantees failure
- When budget can't sustain the channel (paid outbound is expensive)
- For one-time transactions with no LTV to justify CAC
- When the message is generic (will damage brand)

## Common pitfalls

- Spraying generic pitches to a poorly-defined audience
- Hammering prospects with too many calls/emails (Pereira's anti-pattern, page 154)
- Selling on first DM instead of building rapport on social
- Cold calling without preliminary research (futile, per Pereira)
- Skipping the segmentation/ICP step and going straight to dialing
- No follow-up plan after first contact (most deals require 5+ touches)
- Treating outbound as a substitute for, not complement to, inbound
- Letting reps optimize for volume instead of meeting/opportunity quality

## Key metrics

- **Outreach volume** — dials/emails/DMs per rep per day
- **Reply rate** — replies / outreach (cold email healthy = 5-15%)
- **Meeting rate** — meetings booked / outreach
- **Opportunity rate** — qualified opps / meetings
- **Sales velocity** — opp value × win rate / cycle length
- **CAC** — by channel and segment

## Decision questions

- Is our ICP sharp enough that we know exactly who to call today?
- Do reps have a research workflow before each outreach, or are they smiling-and-dialing?
- Are our cadences multi-channel or single-channel (phone-only is dead)?
- How many touches before a rep gives up? (Most stop at 2; deals close at 5+)
- Are we tracking reply quality, or just volume?
- Have we paired outbound with inbound, or are we running them as competitors?

## Canonical examples

- **Salesforce early years** — outbound-heavy SDR motion built the category
- **Most B2B SaaS SDR teams** — outbound is the standard early-stage motion
- **Ben Raffi's six-step outbound system** (page 58)
- **Shopify hybrid** — combined inbound content with outbound prospecting (pages 25-27)
- **Live chat as outbound** (page 43) — 48% of customers expect it
