# ExO Canvas

## Definition

The ExO Canvas is a design framework that complements the Business Model Canvas (BMC) by forcing the team to plan how exponential leverage will be created — not just how value flows. Pereira presents it as the bridge between an MTP and a working business model: while the BMC captures customer segments, value propositions, channels, revenue streams, and the cost structure, the ExO Canvas captures the five external-leverage attributes that make a business *exponential* rather than merely well-designed. Used together, the two canvases ensure that a venture has both a coherent value-flow story (BMC) and a coherent leverage story (ExO Canvas). The author's prescription is iterative: define the BMC blocks, then validate each against the corresponding ExO attribute, and oscillate between the two until they reinforce each other.

## Key attributes

- **Pairs with the Business Model Canvas** — neither canvas is sufficient alone (page 83)
- **Anchored on the MTP** — every attribute serves the Massive Transformative Purpose (page 81)
- **Five external-leverage attributes (SCALE)** — Staff on Demand, Community & Crowd, Algorithms, Leveraged Assets, Engagement (pages 84-85)
- **Asset-orchestration mindset** — leveraged assets are accessed, not owned (page 85)
- **Algorithmic substitution** — work currently done by partners/employees is reviewed for algorithmic replacement (page 85)
- **Engagement as a moat** — sustained relationships with customers, users, key partners, suppliers (page 85)
- **Iterative dialog with BMC** — define one, validate against the other, move back and forth (page 85)
- **Four-of-five rule of thumb** — using at least four ExO attributes (plus MTP) materially raises odds of exponential growth (page 83)

## When to use

- Designing a new exponential venture from scratch (ExO from day one)
- Designing an "edge" business inside an established organization that is trying to escape its immune system
- Stress-testing an existing BMC to ask "where is our leverage?"
- Coaching a team through the difference between a *good* business and an *exponential* one
- Mapping competitors and asking which ExO attributes they exploit and which they ignore

## When NOT to use

- The business is genuinely linear by design (artisanal craft, regulated specialist services) — applying ExO Canvas creates pressure to fake leverage that does not exist
- The team has not yet defined an MTP — without it, the canvas has nothing to orient toward and devolves into a checklist
- As a substitute for the BMC — it complements but does not replace the BMC; on its own it ignores cost structure and revenue logic

## Common pitfalls

- **Filling every box for completeness** — forcing all five attributes when only two genuinely apply produces a fictional model
- **Treating Staff on Demand as outsourcing** — it is a deeper architectural shift toward orchestrated talent, not a cost-cutting tactic
- **Confusing Community & Crowd with marketing** — community is a co-production force, not a distribution channel
- **Skipping the MTP** — the canvas becomes purely tactical without a purpose to organize the leverage around
- **Ignoring Engagement** — the easy attribute to under-resource; without it the other four leak value over time
- **Designing in isolation from the BMC** — leverage without a revenue model is innovation theater

## Key metrics

- **Number of SCALE attributes meaningfully populated** — target ≥4 of 5 (page 83)
- **Share of operations algorithmically handled** — proxy for the Algorithms attribute
- **Asset orchestration ratio** — assets accessed via partners ÷ assets owned outright
- **Community contribution rate** — share of value or content created by non-employee community members
- **Engagement depth metric** — DAU/MAU, NPS, contribution per active user

## Decision questions

- What is our MTP, and does every attribute on this canvas serve it?
- Which functions currently performed by employees or partners could become algorithmic instead?
- What assets are critical to our value proposition that we do not need to own?
- Who is our community, and what do they get from contributing — not just what we get from them?
- How do we measure engagement, and is it actually trending in the right direction?
- For each ExO attribute we leave blank, what is our honest answer for why?

## Canonical examples

- **Airbnb ExO Canvas** — referenced as the worked example throughout the design chapter (pages 80-83): MTP = belonging anywhere; Leveraged Assets = rooms owned by hosts; Community & Crowd = host/guest community; Algorithms = matching, pricing, ranking; Engagement = reviews, superhost status
- **Uber** — Staff on Demand (drivers), Algorithms (matching, surge pricing), Engagement (ratings) (pages 72-73)
- **Waze** — Community & Crowd at the core; entire dataset is user-contributed (page 38)
- **StitchFix** — Algorithms attribute extreme: 65+ data scientists driving almost every operational decision (page 40)
- **Zappos** — Networked Culture as an Engagement pattern (holacracy) (page 41)
