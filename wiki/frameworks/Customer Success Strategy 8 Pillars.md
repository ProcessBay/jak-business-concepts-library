# Customer Success Strategy 8 Pillars

## Definition

Pereira's eight-pillar Customer Success Strategy is a lifecycle framework that maps the deliberate work required across the entire customer relationship — from first impression to renewal and expansion. Unlike a one-time onboarding plan or a churn-prevention checklist, it treats customer success as a continuous flywheel covering: Brand Awareness → Product/Service Education → Customer Acquisition → Guided Onboarding → Customer Support → Value Realization → Customer Loyalty → Expansion Offers. The strategy's premise is that customer success cannot be siloed in a CSM team: every department (marketing, sales, support, product, operations) is "pulling in the same direction" around customer outcomes. Each pillar is sequenced, but they also run in parallel for the customer base at large — different cohorts are at different stages simultaneously.

## Key attributes

- **Lifecycle-shaped** — covers pre-purchase through advocacy, not just post-sale
- **Cross-functional ownership** — marketing owns awareness/education, sales owns acquisition, CSM owns onboarding/value, support owns issue resolution, all own loyalty
- **Sequential AND parallel** — sequential per-customer, parallel across the cohort
- **Onboarding as inflection point** — the highest-leverage stage in B2B (page 36)
- **Value Realization as the anchor** — the moment that justifies every prior investment (page 37)
- **Expansion is earned, not asked** — Pereira positions expansion as the eighth pillar, after loyalty
- **Strategy, not a checklist** — each pillar requires deliberate processes, ownership, and metrics
- **Compounds with brand** — awareness improves acquisition quality, which makes every later pillar easier

## When to use

- Designing a Customer Success function from scratch in a subscription or B2B services business
- Auditing an existing CSM organization for gaps across the lifecycle
- Aligning marketing, sales, product, and CS around a single retention narrative
- Building a customer success roadmap when leadership only thinks "post-sale"
- Diagnosing why retention is failing despite a strong product (often an upstream pillar gap)

## When NOT to use

- One-time transactional businesses with no renewal cycle
- Very early stage when there's no cohort to manage yet (focus on one customer first)
- When the org is too small to staff multiple pillars (compress to 3-4 core pillars)
- As a rigid stage-gate (it's a lens, not a process discipline)

## Common pitfalls

- Treating onboarding as paperwork instead of the highest-leverage stage
- Skipping Value Realization measurement — assuming customers "must be" getting value
- Asking for expansion before loyalty is proven (Pillar 8 before Pillar 7)
- Brand Awareness work owned by marketing in isolation, not informed by CSM insights
- Customer Support as the only post-sale touchpoint (collapses Pillars 4-8 into one)
- No single owner for each pillar's metrics
- Strategy lives in a deck, not in operating cadences

## Key metrics

- **Brand Awareness** — qualified inbound, branded search, organic traffic
- **Education** — content engagement, certification rates (HubSpot Academy model)
- **Acquisition** — CAC, conversion rate, sales-CSM handoff quality score
- **Onboarding** — time-to-first-value, onboarding completion rate, early churn
- **Support** — first-contact resolution, CSAT, ticket volume
- **Value Realization** — % of customers who hit defined value milestones, NPS
- **Loyalty** — NRR, GRR, advocacy rate, referrals
- **Expansion** — net expansion revenue, cross-sell rate, account growth

## Decision questions

- Which of the eight pillars is our weakest — and what's the cost of leaving it weak?
- Who owns each pillar end-to-end (not "everyone")?
- Are we measuring Value Realization, or just assuming it?
- Are our expansion asks coming before or after proven loyalty?
- Does our onboarding match the leverage Pereira ascribes to it?

## Canonical examples

- **HubSpot** — full-stack execution: marketing blog + Academy (education) → freemium (acquisition) → guided onboarding → knowledge base (support) → upsell to higher tiers (expansion) (pages 47-48)
- **Salesforce** — Trailhead (education) + Customer Success Group (onboarding/value) + AppExchange (expansion)
- **Gainsight** — sells the platform that operationalizes these pillars; eats its own dog food (page 12)
- **HelpCrunch** — onboarding-first execution: welcome email, follow-ups, how-to guides, automated comms (pages 45-46)
