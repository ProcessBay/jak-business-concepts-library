# PAS Framework

## Definition

PAS — Problem, Agitate, Solution — is a three-step persuasion framework for sales pitches, landing pages, cold emails, and direct-response copy. Step one names the prospect's most pressing problem in their own language. Step two agitates the problem by spelling out the consequences of inaction — the lost revenue, the wasted time, the competitive exposure. Step three positions the offering as the most effective resolution. Pereira presents PAS as one of the strongest pitch structures for SaaS sales because it forces the rep or marketer to start from the buyer's pain rather than from the product's features. Critically, Pereira warns that PAS is not about manufacturing fake problems or fear-mongering — done right, it surfaces real consequences the prospect was already half-aware of and makes them concrete enough to demand action. PAS pairs naturally with the "create urgency to change" principle that drives the buyer past status-quo bias.

## Key attributes

- **Three steps** — Problem → Agitate → Solution
- **Problem in buyer's language** — uses the words the prospect uses, not internal jargon
- **Agitation = consequence-naming** — not fear-mongering, but making cost-of-inaction tangible
- **Solution at the end** — product enters only after pain is established
- **Works across media** — cold email, landing page, demo opener, sales deck
- **Counter to feature-dump pitches** — leads with pain, not with product
- **Anti-pattern: invented problems** — agitating non-existent pain destroys trust
- **Pairs with stories and proof** — strongest when consequences are illustrated by case examples

## When to use

- Cold email and outbound sequences (PAS opener outperforms feature-led)
- Landing-page hero sections for direct-response acquisition
- The first 90 seconds of a discovery call
- Sales deck openers (before any company-overview slide)
- Pitch competitions and investor narratives (problem-led storytelling)
- Marketing campaigns to status-quo-biased buyers

## When NOT to use

- When the prospect has not yet experienced the problem (Awareness-stage buyers — insight-led works better)
- For commodity purchases where pain is obvious and agitation feels patronizing
- In categories where regulatory or industry sensitivity makes "agitation" feel manipulative
- Internal communications and executive-level pitches (often want signal, not story)
- When you cannot back the solution claim with proof — PAS without proof reads as snake oil

## Common pitfalls

- Manufacturing problems the prospect does not actually have (Pereira's explicit warning, page 76)
- Fear-based agitation that crosses into manipulation
- Skipping the agitation step (Problem-then-Solution loses persuasive lift)
- Generic problems ("you want more sales") instead of specific ones
- Solution that doesn't actually resolve the agitated problem
- Overuse — every email opening with PAS becomes formulaic and recognizable
- No social proof or case study to back the Solution claim

## Key metrics

- **Email reply rate** — PAS vs. control subject lines/openers
- **Landing-page conversion** — bounce rate, scroll depth, form submit
- **Demo request rate** off pitch decks using PAS structure
- **Reply sentiment** — does the prospect engage on the named pain?
- **A/B test lift** — PAS vs. feature-led copy (typically 10-30% lift when done well)

## Decision questions

- Is the problem we named one the prospect would say in their own words?
- Are we agitating real consequences, or inventing fear?
- Does our Solution actually resolve the Agitated Problem (or pivot to something else)?
- Have we earned the right to agitate — i.e., do we have proof we can solve it?
- Are we using PAS as one tool, or as our only tool (formulaic risk)?
- Would a sophisticated reader recognize this as PAS and tune out?

## Canonical examples

- **Direct-response copywriting** — Dan Kennedy, Gary Halbert, Eugene Schwartz lineage
- **Modern SaaS landing pages** — most pain-led hero sections follow PAS implicitly
- **HubSpot, Drift, Gong cold emails** — PAS-structured outbound has dominated B2B since ~2015
- **Pereira's own SaaS pitch example** (pages 75-76) — frames PAS as the urgency-creation lever for software sales
