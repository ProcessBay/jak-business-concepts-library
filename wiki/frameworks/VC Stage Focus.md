# VC Stage Focus

## Definition

Venture capital firms specialize by the lifecycle stage at which they deploy capital, and this stage choice cascades into check size, fund size, ownership target, value-add model, and exit timeline. Pereira's catalog of 100 firms makes the segmentation legible: pure seed/accelerator firms (Y Combinator, 500 Global, SOSV) write small checks across large cohorts; early-stage firms (First Round, Initialized, Founders Fund) lead Series A/B rounds; multi-stage generalists (Sequoia, Andreessen Horowitz, NEA, Insight Partners) follow companies from seed through growth; late-stage and growth specialists (TCV, Tiger Global, IVP, Founders Circle, Addition) write large checks into companies with proven traction. A firm's stage focus is the single biggest determinant of its operating model — a $700M seed fund and a $20B growth fund are different businesses despite both being called "VC."

## Key attributes

- **Stage bands** — Pre-seed/Accelerator → Seed → Series A/B (Early) → Series C+ (Growth) → Pre-IPO (Late)
- **Check size scales with stage** — accelerators $25K-$500K; seed $500K-$3M; Series A $5M-$15M; growth $20M-$100M+; late-stage $100M+
- **Fund size follows check size** — $50M seed funds vs $20B growth funds (TCV, Tiger Global, Insight)
- **Ownership target falls with stage** — seed leads target 15-25%; growth investors accept 3-10%
- **Value-add shifts** — early-stage = product/PMF/hiring help; growth = scale-ops, IPO prep, secondary liquidity
- **Cycle time differs** — seed funds have 7-10 year DPI horizons; late-stage may exit in 18-36 months
- **Follow-on reserves** — early funds reserve 50-70% of capital for follow-on; pure growth funds rarely follow on into earlier rounds

## When to use (as a founder choosing investors)

- Match stage to your true milestone (don't take a growth check at seed and inherit the dilution)
- Pick early-stage specialists when you need product/team/PMF help, not just capital
- Pick growth specialists when scaling is the dominant risk and capital is the dominant input
- Use accelerators when you need community, structured curriculum, and signal value (YC, 500, SOSV)
- Stack a tier-1 early lead with a strong growth follow-on in later rounds

## When NOT to use

- Don't take late-stage money at seed — the valuation and governance terms misfit the stage
- Don't take a seed-stage lead into a growth round — they lack the operating muscle and pro-rata firepower
- Avoid generalists who claim "any stage" without a stage-specific team
- Avoid accelerator-only firms if you don't need the structured cohort program

## Common pitfalls

- Confusing fund AUM with check size (a $20B fund may write $50M minimums — too big for a Series A)
- Taking signal-only money from a tier-1 growth firm without their real engagement
- Misreading "early-stage focus" — Insight Partners and Tiger Global brand as multi-stage but cluster heavy at growth
- Ignoring reserves — a fund that's depleted its follow-on reserves can't defend pro-rata in your next round
- Stage-drift over a fund's life (Founders Fund moved from seed to multi-stage; check current behavior, not founding story)

## Key metrics

- **Check size range** — what they typically write at lead, follow, and pro-rata
- **Fund size and vintage** — current fund's size and how much is deployed
- **Ownership target** — what % they aim to own post-round
- **Reserve ratio** — % of fund held for follow-ons
- **Time-to-DPI** — historical cycle from investment to distribution
- **MOIC / IRR / TVPI** — fund-level returns by vintage
- **Lead vs follow ratio** — % of deals where they lead vs follow another lead

## Decision questions

- What stage is this firm's *most recent* fund actually deploying at (not their founding story)?
- Is their check size right for our round, or will they over- or under-invest?
- Do they have enough reserves to follow us into the next two rounds?
- Does the partner who'd lead our deal have stage-appropriate operating experience?
- If we take their money now, can we still attract the right tier-1 lead at the next stage?
