# Customer Value Creation Model

## Definition

The customer value creation model is a six-step methodology for building offerings that systematically meet specific customer needs: (1) identify target customers, (2) determine value proposition, (3) define customer experience, (4) identify value drivers, (5) continuously improve. Pereira's framework departs from product-out thinking — it starts with the customer and works backward to the offering. The illustrative example is Airbnb, which built a unique platform connecting travelers with affordable, unique accommodations by deeply understanding what specific travelers wanted that hotels weren't providing.

## Key attributes

- **Customer-first sequence** — target audience and needs come BEFORE product design
- **Five sequential steps** — Identify Target Customer → Value Proposition → Customer Experience → Value Drivers → Continuous Improvement
- **Persona-driven** — relies on demographic, psychographic, and behavioral customer personas
- **Multi-source research** — combines market research, customer surveys, sales/service feedback, web analytics, competitor analysis
- **Iterative** — Step 5 (continuous improvement) folds new feedback back into Steps 1-4
- **Benefit-focused** — emphasizes benefits delivered to customer, not features of product

## When to use

- Designing a new offering or entering a new market
- Repositioning a stagnant existing product
- After losing share to a customer-centric competitor
- When customer feedback consistently surfaces gaps the product doesn't address
- During strategic resets where leadership has lost touch with customer reality

## When NOT to use

- Late-stage scaling where customer needs are well understood and execution is the bottleneck
- Pure operational efficiency problems unrelated to customer value
- When customer research budget doesn't exist (use leaner customer-development methods)
- B2B markets where the customer is a procurement function and the buyer differs from the user (need stakeholder mapping first)

## Common pitfalls

- Doing Step 1 (target customer) once and never revisiting — customers evolve
- Defining value proposition in feature-language instead of customer-benefit language
- Skipping Step 3 (customer experience) and treating product as the whole offering
- Treating continuous improvement as wishful thinking rather than operationalized feedback loops
- Conflating "what customers say they want" with "what they will pay for and use" (declared vs. revealed preference)

## Key metrics

- NPS / CSAT on the offering
- Customer retention and repeat purchase rate
- Customer lifetime value
- Time-to-value for new customers
- Insight-to-implementation cycle time (Step 5)

## Decision questions

- Can we name our specific target customer in one sentence, with their specific need?
- What benefit (not feature) does our offering deliver, in the customer's own words?
- What is the single weakest moment in the customer's experience with us right now?
- What value driver are we measurably advancing this quarter?
- How does new customer feedback actually change what we build — show the loop.

## Canonical examples

- **Airbnb** — used the customer value creation model to understand travelers' needs and create a platform offering unique, affordable accommodations not served by hotels (pages 17, 41)
- **Amazon** — customer-centric approach: fast delivery, personalized recommendations, hassle-free returns, one-click ordering (page 22)
- **Apple** — clean and intuitive device interface, personalized App Store recommendations, Genius Bar customer service, AppleCare ecosystem (page 23)
- **Starbucks** — customization options, mobile-app personalized recommendations, rewards program, store atmosphere (page 23)
