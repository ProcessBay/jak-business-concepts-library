# SaaS Company Stages

## Definition

The four-stage lifecycle of a SaaS company: Pre-startup, Startup, Growth, and Maturity. Each stage has distinct activities, risks, metrics focus, and funding needs. Pereira: "Every SaaS company's long-term goal is to achieve scale, but premature scaling can lead the company, especially the startups, to fail. Understanding the stages helps companies to identify when is the perfect time to expand and know when is the right time to focus on scale."

## Stage 1: Pre-startup

**Where you are:** Idea stage. No product, no customers, no revenue.

**Key question:** "What problem are we wanting to solve and is there a demand for the solution among probable customers?"

**Activities:**
- Research and customer discovery interviews
- Building business relationships and partnerships
- Securing initial funding (founder capital, angels, grants)
- Developing Minimum Viable Product (MVP)

**Metrics focus:**
- # of customer interviews conducted
- Problem-solution fit signals
- MVP development progress
- Cash runway

**Risks:**
- Failure to design a business model that enables future profitability
- Dependency on initial funds longer than expected
- Insufficient funds for infrastructure and employment costs
- Building a solution in search of a problem

**Funding:** Pre-seed, founder capital, grants, accelerators

**Team:** 1-3 founders

---

## Stage 2: Startup

**Where you are:** Product launched. First customers acquired. Limited resources.

**Key question:** "Can we acquire and satisfy our first customers while building a repeatable process?"

**Activities:**
- Receiving and acting on customer feedback
- Polishing product features based on feedback
- Implementing metrics and analytics
- Hiring first employees
- Securing seed funding

**Metrics focus:**
- # of paying customers
- Activation rate
- Early churn signals
- CAC (even if high)
- MRR (even if small)

**Risks:**
- Failure to identify ideal customer profile
- Spending more than anticipated on customer acquisition
- Early hiring mistakes (wrong people, wrong roles)
- Running out of cash before finding product-market fit

**Funding:** Seed round, angel investors, early VC

**Team:** 3-15 people

---

## Stage 3: Growth

**Where you are:** Customers are rolling in. Business shows signs of sustainability.

**Key question:** "How do we scale customer acquisition while maintaining retention and unit economics?"

**Activities:**
- Securing Series A funding (growth capital)
- Scaling back-end infrastructure
- Improving conversion rates
- Driving new customer acquisition systematically
- Building out sales and marketing functions

**Metrics focus:**
- MRR growth rate (10-20% MoM)
- LTV:CAC ratio (>3:1)
- Churn rate (stabilizing)
- Net Revenue Retention (>100%)
- Unit economics (payback period <12 months)

**Risks:**
- Competitors copying the business model
- Insufficient funds to sustain growth
- Scaling too early (premature scaling)
- Losing focus on product quality as growth accelerates

**Funding:** Series A, B (growth capital)

**Team:** 15-100 people

---

## Stage 4: Maturity

**Where you are:** Growth slowing. Market knows your product. Brand established.

**Key question:** "How do we defend market position, expand globally, and extract maximum value?"

**Activities:**
- Adding new products/services to pipeline
- Developing new features for existing products
- Investing in R&D for additional growth
- Considering IPO or strategic acquisition
- Global expansion with local agents/partners

**Metrics focus:**
- Market share
- Net Revenue Retention (>115%)
- Gross margin expansion
- Operating leverage (revenue growing faster than costs)
- Rule of 40 (growth rate + profit margin ≥ 40%)

**Risks:**
- Less focus on monitoring market for necessary changes
- Stopping investment in UX
- Complacency leading to disruption by new entrants
- Innovation stagnation

**Funding:** Series C+, IPO, private equity, or acquisition

**Team:** 100+ people

## Stage transition signals

| From | To | Signal |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-startup → Startup | MVP built; first paying customer |
| Startup → Growth | MRR growing 10%+ MoM; LTV:CAC > 2:1; churn stabilizing |
| Growth → Maturity | Growth rate declining; market share established; considering IPO |

## Decision questions by stage

**Pre-startup:**
- Have we validated that the problem is painful enough that people will pay?
- Do we have 10 people who say they would buy this?
- What's our runway, and what's the milestone that triggers next funding?

**Startup:**
- What's our activation rate, and are customers returning?
- Which acquisition channel is showing early signs of repeatability?
- Are we building what customers want, or what we think they want?

**Growth:**
- What's our unit economics, and do they improve at scale?
- Should we focus on new customer acquisition or expansion revenue?
- Are we scaling the team and infrastructure ahead of demand, or behind it?

**Maturity:**
- What's our defensive moat against new entrants?
- Should we expand geographically or vertically?
- Is it time to consider M&A, IPO, or strategic partnership?

## Common pitfalls across stages

- **Pre-startup:** Building before validating
- **Startup:** Optimizing for vanity metrics instead of retention
- **Growth:** Premature scaling (hiring sales before product-market fit)
- **Maturity:** Complacency and innovation stagnation
