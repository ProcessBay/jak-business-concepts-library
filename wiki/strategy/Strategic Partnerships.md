# Strategic Partnerships

## Definition

Strategic partnerships are formal alliances between two or more organizations to achieve mutual business objectives that would be difficult or impossible to achieve independently. Unlike transactional vendor relationships, strategic partnerships involve shared goals, joint investment, and mutual risk. Pereira: "Strategic partnerships are not about outsourcing — they are about combining complementary strengths to create value that neither partner could create alone."

## Types of strategic partnerships

| Type | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Integration partnership** | Technical integration between products | Slack + Salesforce |
| **Channel partnership** | One partner sells/delivers the other's product | AWS + Consulting firms |
| **Co-marketing partnership** | Joint marketing campaigns | Spotify + Uber |
| **Supply partnership** | Shared supply chain or resources | Airlines + Fuel suppliers |
| **R&D partnership** | Joint research and development | Pharma + Biotech |
| **Strategic investment** | Equity investment with strategic intent | Google + Uber (historical) |
| **Joint venture** | New entity created by partners | Sony + Ericsson (historically) |

## Partnership lifecycle

1. **Identification** — Map ecosystem; identify gaps and complementary strengths
2. **Evaluation** — Assess strategic fit, cultural alignment, and mutual value
3. **Negotiation** — Define terms, commitments, governance, and exit clauses
4. **Integration** — Technical, operational, and cultural integration
5. **Management** — Ongoing governance, performance tracking, and relationship maintenance
6. **Evolution or exit** — Scale, renegotiate, or wind down

## When to use

- Entering a new market where a partner has existing presence
- Building platform ecosystem (APIs, integrations, marketplace)
- Scaling distribution without building direct sales
- Accessing technology or IP without acquisition
- Sharing risk for large or uncertain opportunities
- Accelerating time-to-market

## When NOT to use

- Core competency is at risk of being commoditized by partner
- Partner has conflicting strategic interests
- Cultural misalignment that cannot be bridged
- Deal creates dependency on a single partner
- Partnership complexity exceeds value created
- Legal/regulatory restrictions on collaboration

## Common pitfalls

- **Misaligned incentives** — partners optimize for different outcomes
- **Power imbalance** — larger partner dominates, smaller partner gets marginalized
- **Integration failure** — technical or operational integration is harder than expected
- **Commitment drift** — initial enthusiasm fades, resources are redirected
- **Channel conflict** — partner competes with direct sales
- **Over-dependence** — too much revenue from one partner creates vulnerability
- **Slow decision-making** — joint governance creates bureaucracy

## Key metrics

| Metric | Definition | Target |
|---|---|---|
| **Partnership Revenue** | Revenue attributable to partner channel | Growing % of total |
| **CAC Reduction** | CAC via partner vs. direct | Lower than direct |
| **Time to Close** | Average deal closure time via partner | Faster than direct |
| **Partner Satisfaction** | Partner's rating of the relationship | >80% satisfied |
| **Joint Customers** | Customers using both partners' products | Growing |

## Decision questions

- What capability gap does this partnership fill?
- What would it cost to build this capability internally vs. partner?
- Do our incentives align, or will we compete for the same customer dollar?
- What happens if the partnership fails — do we have alternatives?
- Who owns the customer relationship — us or the partner?
- What is the exit strategy if the partnership underperforms?
- Does this partnership strengthen or weaken our strategic position?

## Canonical examples

- **Microsoft + Intel (Wintel)** — Dominant PC ecosystem partnership for decades
- **Salesforce + AppExchange** — Platform partnership model enabling thousands of integrations
- **Airbnb + Google Maps** — Integration partnership enhancing user experience
- **Stripe + Shopify** — Payment integration enabling e-commerce growth
