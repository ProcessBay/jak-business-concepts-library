# Customer Conversion Rate

## Definition

Customer conversion rate is the percentage of interested prospects or website visitors who complete a desired action — most commonly becoming a paying customer. It measures the efficiency of turning demand into revenue and is a critical bridge between top-of-funnel activity and actual sales.

## Key attributes

- **Formula:** Conversion Rate = (Number of Conversions / Total Visitors or Leads) × 100.
- **Context-dependent:** A "conversion" can mean a purchase, a trial signup, a form fill, a newsletter subscription, or a demo request depending on business goals.
- **Funnel stage:** Conversion can be measured at multiple stages — ad click to landing page, landing page to trial, trial to paid, etc.
- **Channel variation:** Different channels (organic, paid, referral, outbound) typically show very different conversion rates.

## When to use

- Evaluating marketing campaign effectiveness.
- A/B testing landing pages, pricing pages, and checkout flows.
- Diagnosing funnel bottlenecks — where are prospects dropping off?
- Comparing channel quality — which sources deliver the highest-intent traffic?

## When NOT to use

- Do not optimize conversion rate without considering customer quality — high conversion with high churn is a trap.
- Do not compare conversion rates across industries without context.

## Common pitfalls

- Optimizing for the wrong conversion event (e.g., signups instead of qualified signups).
- Ignoring mobile vs. desktop conversion differences.
- Not tracking multi-touch attribution — the "converting" touchpoint may not be the only influential one.

## Decision questions

1. What is our primary conversion event, and does it correlate with long-term customer value?
2. Which funnel stage has the biggest drop-off?
3. How do conversion rates vary by acquisition channel?
4. What is the marginal impact of a 1% conversion improvement on revenue?
